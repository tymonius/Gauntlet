# Gauntlet v0.6 Rules Cleanup

**Status:** Applies to the v0.6 working rules unless superseded by a later v0.6 rule update.  
**Purpose:** Capture low-risk v0.6 cleanup items without pulling the broader Diplomat economy overhaul into v0.6.

---

## Withdrawal

When a player **withdraws**, that player moves one space away from the opposing player, toward their own Heartland, if able.

If the **attacker withdraws**, the attacker returns to the space they entered from, if able.

If the **defender withdraws**, the defender leaves the battle space and moves one space toward their own Heartland, if able. The attacker remains in or occupies the battle space.

If **both players withdraw**, move the attacker first, then move the defender. If one player cannot withdraw, the other player still withdraws if able.

A withdrawal is not a retreat unless a rule specifically says otherwise.

If an effect says that a player remains in or occupies the battle space, that specific instruction overrides the general withdrawal procedure.

## Diplomat Proposal Name Cleanup

The first two Diplomat Proposals should be renamed for clarity.

### 1. De-escalation

This is the Proposal formerly named **Orderly Withdrawal**.

**Cost:** 0 Influence

**If Terms are accepted:**  
Both players withdraw. You gain 1 Influence. No battle occurs.

**If Terms are refused:**  
You draw one card. The battle occurs normally.

### 2. Orderly Withdrawal

This is the Proposal formerly named **De-escalation**.

**Cost:** 0 Influence  
**Requirement:** You must be attacking.

**If Terms are accepted:**  
You withdraw one space. The opponent remains in or occupies the battle space. The opponent draws one card. You gain 2 Influence. No battle occurs.

**If Terms are refused:**  
You gain 1 Influence. The battle occurs normally.

## Assimilation Destination Cleanup

If **Assimilation** causes an immediate capture, send Assimilation to the Graveyard after the capture resolves.

If Assimilation does not cause an immediate capture, it follows its normal destination.

This keeps the immediate-capture shortcut powerful while making the capture acceleration costly.

---

## Condition Reduction Pass

After the card-by-card v0.6 review is complete, audit every card and rule that creates a **Condition** and remove as many Conditions as practical.

Preferred replacements:

- Use an **Asset** when the effect belongs to a player, remains available until used, and should compete for Asset-bank space.
- Use an **Overlay** when the effect belongs visibly to a specific Territory.
- Resolve and discard the card immediately when no persistent component is necessary.
- Keep a Condition only when neither an Asset nor Overlay cleanly represents the effect and the persistent timing is genuinely necessary.

Goals:

- Reduce loose cards sitting beside the board without a clear home.
- Make persistent effects easier to read at a glance.
- Reduce forgotten triggers and unclear ownership.
- Prefer optional, visible use over forced “the next time” triggers where appropriate.

Do not convert effects mechanically just to eliminate the word **Condition**. Any conversion must preserve pacing, counterplay, Asset-bank pressure, and board-state clarity.

## v0.6.1 Hold

Do not use this cleanup pass to overhaul the Diplomat economy.

The following items are held for **v0.6.1 — Diplomat faction overhaul**:

- Proposal costs as staked Influence.
- Loss of Face replacement or redesign.
- Full Proposal recalibration around accept/refuse incentives.
