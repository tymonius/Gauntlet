## Core-rule summary

### Blasphemy

Whenever the opponent plays a card with the **Arcane** trait, gain 1 Conviction. This does not count toward the normal once-per-turn gain limit, but cannot exceed the maximum.

The Arcane trait is distinct from Arcane faction allegiance.

### Revised 1-Conviction Purge

When using the 1-Conviction Purge, choose one:

- send the top card of the opponent's discard pile to their Graveyard; or
- choose up to two cards in their discard pile with a combined deckbuilding value of 2 or less and send them to their Graveyard.

The remaining Purge options are:

| Cost | Effect |
|---:|---|
| 2 | Choose one opposing Asset and send it to its owner's Graveyard. |
| 3 | The opponent chooses one card from hand and sends it to their Graveyard. |
| 4 | Look at the opponent's hand. Choose one card and send it to their Graveyard. |

# Cost 1

## Accusation

**Cost:** 1  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Coercion; discard control; Purification pressure

> **Action:** Choose one card in the opponent's discard pile. They choose whether to put it on top of their deck or send it to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose one card in the opponent's discard pile. They choose whether to put it on top of their deck or send it to their Graveyard.

Accusation forces the opponent to accept permanent condemnation or allow the Inquisition to shape their next draw. It is useful independently, scales naturally against both compact and oversized decks, and may create Conviction through the normal post-battle trigger without guaranteeing resource generation.

# Cost 2

## Confession

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Forced disclosure; commitment control; adaptive response

> **Action:** Look at the opponent's hand. Choose one Battle card. Until end of turn, if they commit a card from hand in a battle involving you, they must commit the chosen card if able.
>
> **Battle:** After both players have selected their Battle cards but before the normal reveal, reveal Confession. Reveal the opponent's hand commitment, if any. You may replace your own hand commitment with a different Battle card from hand. If you do, return the original card to your hand and play the replacement face up.

Confession uses special-reveal timing. The Action coerces a particular commitment without forcing the opponent to commit at all. The Battle effect permits one informed revision of the Inquisition player's hand commitment without creating an additional Battle-card play.

## Penance

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Coercion; hand attrition; Conviction pressure

> **Action:** The opponent chooses one: send one card from their hand to their Graveyard; or you gain 1 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** After Battle cards are revealed, the opponent chooses one: send one card from their hand to their Graveyard; or add +1 to your battle total.

Penance gives the opponent agency while ensuring that every available choice advances either permanent punishment or immediate Inquisition strength.

## Divine Mercy

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Condemnation reversal; resource conversion; immediate versus long-term value

> **Action:** Choose one card in the opponent's Graveyard and move it to their discard pile. Gain 2 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** Choose one card in the opponent's Graveyard and move it to their discard pile. Add +2 to your battle total.

Divine Mercy allows the Inquisition to reverse part of its own permanent progress in exchange for immediate authority or force. It is intentionally powerful and is on the package balance watchlist for automatic-inclusion pressure.

# Cost 3

## No Martyrs

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Defeat-trigger suppression; retreat pressure; conventional victory

> **Action:** Bank No Martyrs as an Asset. After Battle cards are revealed in a battle involving you, you may discard No Martyrs. If you do and the opponent loses that battle, they cannot benefit from effects they control that trigger because they lost or retreated from that battle, and they retreat one additional space.
>
> **Battle:** If the opponent loses this battle, they cannot benefit from effects they control that trigger because they lost or retreated from this battle, and they retreat one additional space.

No Martyrs suppresses beneficial loss and retreat triggers without preventing ordinary retreat or harmful consequences. Its reliable floor is stronger territorial pressure; its ceiling rises as additional factions introduce profitable-loss and recovery effects.

## Excommunication

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Mass condemnation; value-scaled removal; Purification pressure

> **Action:** Choose one or more cards in the opponent's discard pile with a combined deckbuilding value of up to 5. Send them to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose one or more cards in the opponent's discard pile with a combined deckbuilding value of up to 3. Send them to their Graveyard.

Excommunication can remove one premium card or several inexpensive cards. Its Battle effect sacrifices immediate battle strength for long-term exhaustion pressure and may produce Conviction through the normal post-battle trigger.

## Guilt by Association

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Duplicate punishment; deck-composition pressure; Purification

> **Action:** Choose a card in the opponent's discard pile. Send it and any other copies of that card in their discard pile to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose a card the opponent played during that battle. Send any copies of that card in their discard pile to their Graveyard.

Copies are cards with the same title. Guilt by Association has a modest floor against singleton-heavy decks and a high ceiling against repeated-card strategies.

## Act of Faith

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Deck examination; selective condemnation; Purification pressure

> **Action:** Reveal up to three cards from the top of the opponent's deck. Choose one and send it to their Graveyard. Discard the others.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, reveal up to two cards from the top of the opponent's deck. Choose one and send it to their Graveyard. Discard the other.

If only one card is revealed, send it to the Graveyard. Act of Faith attacks hidden resources directly while returning most revealed cards to the recyclable discard pile.

# Cost 4

## Tyranny

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Targeted suppression; Conviction allocation; prepared control

> **Action:** Bank Tyranny as an Asset. Once per turn, after Battle cards are revealed in a battle involving you, you may spend 1 Conviction. If you do, choose one opposing played Battle card and negate it.
>
> **Battle:** Choose one opposing played Battle card and negate it.

Tyranny is direct authoritarian control. Its Asset form requires an Action, an Asset slot, and repeated Conviction expenditure; its Battle form provides one decisive targeted negation.

## Burning at the Stake

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Hand exposure; premium-card punishment; Arcane persecution

> **Action:** The opponent reveals their hand. Send the card with the highest deckbuilding value to their Graveyard. If two or more cards are tied for highest, choose one. If the condemned card has the Arcane trait, gain 1 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** If the opponent loses this battle, they reveal their hand. Send the card with the highest deckbuilding value to their Graveyard. If two or more cards are tied for highest, choose one. If the condemned card has the Arcane trait, gain 1 Conviction.

If the opponent has no cards in hand, the effect does nothing. The Battle mode is severe but contributes nothing toward securing the required victory.

# Cost 5

## Heresy

**Cost:** 5  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Trait:** Arcane  
**Primary threads:** Forbidden appropriation; matchup-dependent toolbox; maximum Conviction commitment

> **Battle:** You may spend 4 Conviction. If you do, choose a card in the opponent's Graveyard with a Battle effect and resolve that effect as though you had played it. That Battle effect may resolve one additional card's Battle effect. The additional effect cannot resolve another Battle effect.

The chosen card remains in the opponent's Graveyard. Heresy creates an explicit one-level exception to the general anti-recursion rule: **Heresy → copied-effect card → final Battle effect** is legal, but the final effect cannot copy or resolve another Battle effect.

## Hellfire

**Cost:** 5  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Flexible Conviction allocation; battle force versus Purification; catastrophic commitment

> **Action:** Spend any amount of Conviction. For each Conviction spent, send the top card of the opponent's deck to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After Battle cards are revealed, spend any amount of Conviction. For each Conviction spent, choose one:
>
> - Add +1 to your battle total.
> - If you win this battle, after the battle send the top card of the opponent's deck to their Graveyard.
>
> You may choose the same option more than once.

Hellfire forces the player to divide stored Conviction between securing the present battle and accelerating the opponent's permanent exhaustion. A Conviction spent on one option does not also provide the other benefit.

## Package audit

### Strategic coverage

The selected pool supports:

- Purification pressure through Accusation, Excommunication, Guilt by Association, Act of Faith, and Hellfire;
- coercion and hand control through Confession, Penance, Burning at the Stake, and Tyranny;
- Conviction conversion through Penance, Divine Mercy, Tyranny, Heresy, and Hellfire;
- punitive battle control through No Martyrs, Tyranny, Burning at the Stake, and Hellfire;
- deliberate reversal of condemnation through Divine Mercy;
- matchup-dependent appropriation through Heresy.

These are overlapping strategic threads rather than sealed packages. A player may pursue literal exhaustion, use Graveyard pressure mainly to constrain the opponent, build around battle control, or repeatedly trade long-term Purification progress for immediate conventional power.

### Leader integration

**Grand Inquisitor** especially values effects that make battle victories permanently destructive and can preserve Conviction for discounted post-victory Purges. Excommunication, Act of Faith, Burning at the Stake, Tyranny, and Hellfire all create meaningful sequencing decisions around Final Judgment.

**Witch Hunter** especially values defensive battle force, denial of recovery, additional retreat, and preserving 2 Conviction for Relentless Pursuit. Penance, Divine Mercy, No Martyrs, Tyranny, and Hellfire naturally support those decisions without becoming unusable for the Grand Inquisitor.

Hellfire is the clearest shared-lens card: either leader may spend all available Conviction, but each has a different reason to preserve part of it.

### Preserved weaknesses

The package does not provide easy self-draw, broad card advantage, routine additional movement, capture acceleration, unconditional Conviction generation, or effortless raw battle superiority. Strong effects require Actions, battle commitments, stored Conviction, opponent losses, exposed zones, or reversal of prior Purification progress.

### Automatic-inclusion review

No card is required for the printed faction or leader rules to function. The package provides multiple competing uses for Conviction rather than repairing the faction's engine.

The primary balance watchlist is:

1. **Divine Mercy** — +2 Battle efficiency and 2-Conviction acceleration may create automatic-inclusion pressure.
2. **Tyranny** — universally useful targeted negation may remain attractive in nearly every construction.
3. **Burning at the Stake** — guaranteed removal of the highest-value hand card may be too punishing in compact premium decks.

### Testing priorities

Test the package against:

- a compact 30-card, high-value deck;
- a mixed-cost deck larger than 30 cards;
- a 60-card deck composed primarily or entirely of cost-1 cards;
- profitable-loss and retreat-trigger strategies;
- Arcane-trait-heavy decks;
- duplicate-heavy and singleton-heavy construction;
- both leaders using the same card pool with different Conviction-reservation priorities.

Track Purification frequency, average cards permanently removed, Conviction gained and spent, Purge selection frequency, Divine Mercy inclusion and use, targeted-negation density, and whether conventional breakthrough remains the better response to oversized low-value decks.
