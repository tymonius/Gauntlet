# Cost 1

## Accusation

**Cost:** 1  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Coercion; discard control; Purification pressure

> **Action:** Choose a card in the opponent's discard pile. They put it on top of their deck or send it to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose a card in the opponent's discard pile. They put it on top of their deck or send it to their Graveyard.

Accusation forces permanent loss or a controlled next draw.

# Cost 2

## Confession

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Forced disclosure; commitment control; adaptive response

> **Action:** Look at the opponent's hand and choose a Battle card. Until end of turn, if they commit from hand in a battle involving you, they must commit that card if able.
>
> **Battle:** After both players select their Battle cards, before reveal, reveal Confession and the opponent's hand commitment, if any. You may return your hand commitment to your hand and replace it with another Battle card from hand, played face up.

Confession uses special-reveal timing and permits one informed replacement, not an additional Battle-card play.

## Penance

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Coercion; hand attrition; Conviction pressure

> **Action:** The opponent chooses one: send a card from hand to their Graveyard; or you gain 1 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** After Battle cards are revealed, the opponent chooses one: send a card from hand to their Graveyard; or you gain +1 battle total.

Penance advances permanent punishment or immediate strength while preserving opponent choice.

## Divine Mercy

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Condemnation reversal; resource conversion; immediate value

> **Action:** Move a card from the opponent's Graveyard to their discard pile. Gain 2 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** Move a card from the opponent's Graveyard to their discard pile. Gain +2 battle total.

Divine Mercy trades permanent progress for immediate power and remains on the balance watchlist.

# Cost 3

## No Martyrs

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Defeat-trigger suppression; retreat pressure; conventional victory

> **Action:** Bank No Martyrs as an Asset. After Battle cards are revealed in a battle involving you, you may discard it. If the opponent loses, they cannot benefit from effects they control triggered by that loss or retreat, and retreat one additional space.
>
> **Battle:** If the opponent loses, they cannot benefit from effects they control triggered by that loss or retreat, and retreat one additional space.

No Martyrs suppresses beneficial loss and retreat triggers without preventing ordinary retreat or harmful effects.

## Excommunication

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Mass condemnation; value-scaled removal; Purification pressure

> **Action:** Choose cards in the opponent's discard pile with combined deckbuilding value up to 5. Send them to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose cards in the opponent's discard pile with combined deckbuilding value up to 3. Send them to their Graveyard.

Excommunication may remove one premium card or several inexpensive cards.

## Guilt by Association

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Duplicate punishment; deck-composition pressure; Purification

> **Action:** Choose a card in the opponent's discard pile. Send every card there with that title to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose a card the opponent played. Send every card in their discard pile with that title to their Graveyard.

This wording defines copies directly by shared title.

## Act of Faith

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Primary threads:** Deck examination; selective condemnation; Purification pressure

> **Action:** Reveal up to three cards from the top of the opponent's deck. Send one to their Graveyard and discard the rest.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, reveal up to two cards from the top of the opponent's deck. Send one to their Graveyard and discard the rest.

If only one card is revealed, send it to the Graveyard.

# Cost 4

## Tyranny

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Targeted suppression; Conviction allocation; prepared control

> **Action:** Bank Tyranny as an Asset. Once per turn, after Battle cards are revealed in a battle involving you, you may spend 1 Conviction to negate one opposing played Battle card.
>
> **Battle:** Negate one opposing played Battle card.

Its Asset form requires an Action, an Asset slot, and Conviction; its Battle form is one-use control.

## Burning at the Stake

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Hand exposure; premium-card punishment; Arcane persecution

> **Action:** The opponent reveals their hand. Send the card with the highest deckbuilding value to their Graveyard; choose among ties. If it has the Arcane trait, gain 1 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** If the opponent loses, they reveal their hand. Send the card with the highest deckbuilding value to their Graveyard; choose among ties. If it has the Arcane trait, gain 1 Conviction.

If the opponent has no cards in hand, the effect does nothing.

# Cost 5

## Heresy

**Cost:** 5  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Trait:** Arcane  
**Primary threads:** Forbidden appropriation; matchup-dependent toolbox; maximum Conviction commitment

> **Battle:** You may spend 4 Conviction to resolve the Battle effect of a card in the opponent's Graveyard as though you played it. That effect may resolve one additional Battle effect; the additional effect cannot resolve another.

The chosen card remains in the opponent's Graveyard. Heresy permits one bounded copied-effect layer.

## Hellfire

**Cost:** 5  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Primary threads:** Flexible Conviction allocation; battle force versus Purification; catastrophic commitment

> **Action:** Spend any amount of Conviction. Send that many cards from the top of the opponent's deck to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After Battle cards are revealed, spend any amount of Conviction. For each Conviction spent, choose one: gain +1 battle total; or, if you win, after the battle send the top card of the opponent's deck to their Graveyard. You may choose either option more than once.

Each Conviction provides only one chosen benefit.

## Package audit

### Strategic coverage

The pool supports:

- Purification pressure through Accusation, Excommunication, Guilt by Association, Act of Faith, and Hellfire;
- coercion and hand control through Confession, Penance, Burning at the Stake, and Tyranny;
- Conviction conversion through Penance, Divine Mercy, Tyranny, Heresy, and Hellfire;
- punitive battle control through No Martyrs, Tyranny, Burning at the Stake, and Hellfire;
- deliberate reversal of condemnation through Divine Mercy;
- matchup-dependent appropriation through Heresy.

These are overlapping tools rather than sealed packages.

### Leader integration

**Grand Inquisitor** values destructive battle victories and preserving Conviction for discounted Purges.  
**Witch Hunter** values defensive strength, extra retreat, and preserving 2 Conviction for Relentless Pursuit.

Hellfire most clearly expresses the shared-pool tension: either leader may spend everything, but each has a different reason to hold some back.

### Preserved weaknesses

The package does not provide easy self-draw, broad card advantage, routine extra movement, capture acceleration, unconditional Conviction generation, or effortless battle superiority.

### Balance watchlist

1. **Divine Mercy** — +2 Battle efficiency and 2-Conviction acceleration.
2. **Tyranny** — repeatable targeted negation and automatic-inclusion pressure.
3. **Burning at the Stake** — severe removal against compact premium decks.

### Testing priorities

Test against:

- compact 30-card premium decks;
- larger mixed-cost decks;
- 60-card cost-1-heavy decks;
- profitable-loss and retreat-trigger strategies;
- Arcane-trait-heavy decks;
- duplicate-heavy and singleton-heavy decks;
- both leaders using the same card pool.

Track Purification frequency, cards permanently removed, Conviction gained and spent, Purge use, Divine Mercy inclusion, targeted-negation density, and conventional breakthrough performance.
