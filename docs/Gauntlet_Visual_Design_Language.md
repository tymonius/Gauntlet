# Gauntlet Visual Design Language

**Status:** Active working design reference.  
**Purpose:** Record approved visual-design principles for Gauntlet cards, components, interfaces, illustrations, and other player-facing materials without replacing production-specific templates or the Leader Design Bible.

The [Leader Design Bible](Gauntlet_v0.6_Leader_Design_Bible.md) governs the character, silhouette, prop, and miniature direction for individual Leaders. This document governs the shared graphic language surrounding that artwork.

---

## 1. Scope

This document establishes the visual rules that should make Gauntlet materials feel like parts of one game while preserving clear faction identity and allowing individual characters to remain visually distinctive.

It applies to:

- playable cards;
- Leader Cards and faction references;
- supplemental faction components;
- Territories and Neutral materials;
- trackers, tokens, and print-and-play sheets;
- the Deckbuilder and other digital interfaces;
- packaging, promotional graphics, and future production materials.

This document records approved decisions. Exact production values, final fonts, final vector assets, and production templates may remain unresolved even when the underlying visual rule has been established.

---

## 2. Faction color principle

> **Faction colors identify the game component; character colors identify the individual, role, and scene.**

Faction colors belong primarily to the graphic system of Gauntlet: card frames, borders, headings, rules, icons, trackers, reference materials, interfaces, and other game components.

A faction color is not a mandatory costume color. Characters do not wear color-coded uniforms merely because they belong to a faction. Their clothing, equipment, environment, and lighting should use whatever palette best expresses the character and scene.

Faction colors may appear within an illustration when they make sense as a natural accent, but they should not be forced into the artwork solely to identify allegiance. The surrounding component design should carry that responsibility.

For example, the Ranger may continue to wear sage green while appearing on an Intelligence Leader Card whose graphic framework is charcoal.

---

## 3. Faction palette

The approved faction color identities are:

| Faction | Graphic color identity |
|---|---|
| Military | Crimson red |
| Diplomats | Royal blue |
| Financiers | Dollar-bill green |
| Mystics | Deep violet |
| Inquisition | Flaming gold |
| Intelligence | Charcoal / near-black |

These descriptions define the intended identity of each palette. Exact production values and supporting tints and shades remain to be standardized.

Faction colors should be used consistently enough that players can identify faction materials at a glance, but they should not overwhelm illustrations or make every component feel like a flat field of saturated color.

### Neutral palette

Neutral cards and faction-neutral components use:

- a pale ivory or off-white outer border;
- the standard warmer natural-parchment interior;
- the same dark inner keyline used throughout the card system.

The ivory should be visibly cleaner and lighter than the parchment interior without reading as a seventh faction color.

---

## 4. Faction emblems

The approved emblem concepts are:

| Faction | Emblem |
|---|---|
| Military | Crossed swords |
| Diplomats | Laurel |
| Financiers | Side-profile coin |
| Mystics | Pentagram |
| Inquisition | Heraldic flame |
| Intelligence | Eye |

The Inquisition flame replaces the previously considered wide-brimmed hat. It should feel severe and heraldic rather than decorative or cozy.

Final emblem assets should be:

- free-standing, single-color symbols;
- readable at small card-icon size;
- consistent in visual weight, negative space, and level of detail;
- usable with or without a surrounding medallion or cartouche.

Faction emblems supplement color recognition and should remain useful in monochrome, color-blind, and low-ink contexts.

---

## 5. Graphic system and illustration system

Gauntlet uses two related but distinct color systems.

### Graphic system

The graphic system communicates game information and component identity. It includes faction colors, typography, borders, rules, badges, icons, labels, and interface treatments.

### Illustration system

The illustration system communicates character, place, atmosphere, material, and narrative. It may draw from a much broader palette than the graphic system.

The two systems should harmonize, but the graphic system should frame the illustration rather than dictate every color within it.

## 6. Shared card system

All playable cards should share one unmistakable Gauntlet structure. Faction identity changes color and may eventually affect restrained ornament, but essential information remains in consistent locations.

### 6.1 Physical format

Ordinary playable cards, Assets, Overlays, and Leader Cards use:

- standard poker size: **2.5 × 3.5 inches**;
- portrait orientation;
- **0.125-inch bleed** on all sides;
- at least **0.125 inches of critical-content safe margin** inside the trim;
- standard rounded corners, approximately **1/8-inch radius**.

Territories use the same physical card size rotated to landscape: **3.5 × 2.5 inches**.

### 6.2 Border and frame

Faction cards use:

- a continuous faction-colored outer border extending through the bleed;
- one universal border width and geometry across all factions;
- a thin dark inner keyline separating the border from the parchment interior.

The border should be substantial enough to identify a faction in a fanned hand without consuming excessive card-face space.

Neutral cards use the same structure with an ivory outer border and warm-parchment interior.

Faction-specific engraved motifs within the border are not approved. The baseline is a solid border, dark inner keyline, and faction emblem. Motifs may be reconsidered only after complete prototypes show that the border system needs additional texture.

### 6.3 Header

Card titles:

- sit on open parchment rather than inside a separate title box;
- use an uppercase display serif;
- use modest tracking;
- remain the dominant header element.

The framed illustration begins beneath the open header and provides the main visual separation. No additional divider is required beneath the title.

The deckbuilding value appears as a distinct upper-right numeric element inside a small neutral medallion or cartouche that may slightly overlap the faction border.

### 6.4 Badges and traits

Mechanically relevant traits and component types use compact text badges in a shared header position. They never rely on an icon alone.

Badge order is:

1. component type, when applicable: **ASSET** or **OVERLAY**;
2. trait, when applicable, such as **ARCANE**.

Examples:

- an ordinary Arcane card displays **ARCANE**;
- an Arcane Asset displays **ASSET · ARCANE**;
- an Arcane Overlay displays **OVERLAY · ARCANE**;
- a card with no type or trait badge gains the extra open space.

Badges do not form a permanent classification line on every card. Only relevant badges appear.

### 6.5 Illustration window

Ordinary playable cards use a defined framed illustration window rather than full-bleed art.

The ordinary-card illustration should occupy roughly the upper **40–45%** of the card. The window should:

- be a broad rectangle that nearly spans the neutral interior;
- use a thin dark keyline;
- use square or only subtly softened corners;
- retain the same basic geometry across ordinary cards.

Illustrations fill their windows without distortion. Cropping may adapt to the source composition.

### 6.6 Rules panel

Ordinary cards use one continuous parchment rules field rather than heavy boxed compartments.

When both effects exist, the fixed order is:

1. **ACTION**
2. **BATTLE**

The sections are separated through typography, spacing, and a thin horizontal rule. If only one effect exists, the absent section disappears and the remaining section expands.

Rules text uses:

- a highly legible serif;
- normal mixed case;
- left alignment;
- comfortable line spacing;
- no full justification.

**ACTION**, **BATTLE**, and similar mechanical labels use compact small capitals. These labels remain textual even if supplemental icons are later added.

Bold is reserved for defined terms, numbers, and genuinely important instructions. Mechanically relevant reminder text may appear when necessary. Flavor text is not part of the default playable-card layout.

### 6.7 Faction-emblem placement

On ordinary faction cards, Assets, and Overlays, the faction emblem occupies a fixed lower-left frame position outside the rules text but within the faction border.

The emblem is secondary to the faction-colored border and never replaces a written mechanical label.

Neutral cards leave this position empty. They do not receive a Neutral or Gauntlet emblem merely to fill the space.

## 7. Leader Cards

Faction Leaders do not receive separate personal colors. A Leader Card inherits the visual identity of its faction, while the Leader's individuality comes from portrait, silhouette, clothing, props, environment, role, and ability.

Leader Cards use the shared poker-card shell with the following changes:

- no deckbuilding-value cartouche;
- a larger framed portrait area, approximately **55%** of the card;
- the same illustration-frame treatment used by ordinary cards;
- the Leader name as the dominant title;
- the faction emblem placed in the header immediately before or beside the faction name;
- no lower-left faction emblem;
- no printed **LEADER** designation;
- no **LEADER ABILITY** label;
- the named ability presented directly above its relevant rules or reference text.

The Leader header hierarchy is:

1. Leader name;
2. faction emblem and faction name.

The lower panel should contain only text relevant to using that Leader. It may accommodate a single ability, multiple named sub-abilities, Orders, or necessary restrictions without changing the overall card structure.

Portrait crops should preserve the full head, including hair or headwear. Excess cropping should come primarily from below rather than above the subject.

---

## 8. Assets

Assets remain portrait-oriented standard playable cards.

An Asset:

- uses the ordinary faction border and parchment shell;
- retains the normal title and upper-right deckbuilding value;
- displays **ASSET** in the first shared badge position;
- displays any trait badge after **ASSET**;
- uses the normal Action-above-Battle rules hierarchy.

The final persistent-in-play visual cue and exact art-to-rules proportion for dense Asset text remain to be standardized through production prototypes.

---

## 9. Overlays

Overlays must function both as ordinary portrait cards in a deck or hand and as landscape components placed over Territories.

### 9.1 Portrait face

In portrait orientation, an Overlay retains the ordinary card structure:

- standard title;
- upper-right deckbuilding value;
- **OVERLAY** in the first badge position;
- any trait badge after **OVERLAY**;
- framed illustration;
- Action and Battle text as applicable;
- faction-colored border or Neutral ivory border.

### 9.2 Landscape title band

Every Overlay includes a full-height vertical title band along the left side in portrait orientation.

The band:

- is approximately **0.4–0.5 inches wide**, or roughly **16–20%** of the portrait card width;
- is substantial enough to read as a secondary title panel rather than a narrow stripe;
- runs the full card height inside the outer border;
- uses the faction color with high-contrast lettering;
- uses ivory with dark lettering on Neutral Overlays;
- repeats only the card title;
- contains no faction emblem;
- contains no cost, rules text, trait, or duplicate **OVERLAY** label;
- centers the repeated title along the full length of the band.

When the card is rotated clockwise onto a Territory, the band becomes a prominent horizontal header across the top of the Overlay.

---

## 10. Territories

Territories use the same **3.5 × 2.5-inch** physical card in landscape orientation and the same ivory-and-parchment faction-neutral materials as Neutral cards.

Territories do not print:

- a **TERRITORY** label;
- a complexity marker;
- an Arena marker;
- a faction or Neutral emblem.

Arena and complexity status are backend metadata only. Arena cards use the same printed Territory template with no special label, icon, frame, or other visible distinction.

### 10.1 Title header

Territory titles:

- sit on open parchment above the framed content area;
- are left-aligned;
- use a fixed-height header across all Territories;
- remain on one line whenever possible;
- may use modest font reduction for long names;
- wrap only as a last resort.

The header has no enclosing box and no separate divider beneath it, matching the shared card system.

### 10.2 Framed content area

Beneath the title, the illustration and rules panel form one shared framed content area.

The illustration fills the available upper portion without distortion. Its vertical crop adapts as the rules panel changes height.

### 10.3 Adaptive bottom rules panel

Territory rules use a full-width parchment panel anchored to the bottom of the framed content area.

The panel:

- uses the same warm parchment as the surrounding card interior;
- is separated from the artwork by a thin movable horizontal divider;
- expands upward only as much as the printed text requires;
- uses fixed side padding, top padding, bottom padding, and paragraph spacing;
- top-aligns the rules text rather than vertically centering short effects;
- uses the same standard serif size and line spacing as ordinary cards.

Short Territory effects therefore leave more room for illustration, while longer effects receive a taller rules panel.

The top edge of the rules panel may not rise above the card's horizontal centerline. If the text cannot fit below that line using standard typography and padding, the rules text must be shortened or rewritten rather than miniaturized.

---

## 11. Card back

All standard playable cards use one universal faction-neutral back so deck contents and faction identity remain hidden.

The back is intentionally directional rather than rotationally symmetric.

The approved composition is:

- a dark neutral field;
- all six faction emblems distributed evenly in a subtle darker tone-on-tone pattern;
- a staggered tessellation in which no faction symbol is larger, brighter, or more frequent than another;
- the Gauntlet wordmark as the dominant directional element.

During setup, when a player lays out unrevealed Territory cards, **GAUNTLET** should read upright from that player's side of the table. After a Territory is flipped, its face text should be upright in the same orientation.

The final wordmark design remains unresolved. Its use on the card back is approved even though the production artwork is not yet final.

---

## 12. Backend-only designations

Complexity is strictly backend metadata. **Basic** and **Advanced** do not appear on any printed or player-facing card, including:

- ordinary cards;
- Assets;
- Overlays;
- Leader Cards;
- Territories;
- Arenas.

Arena status is also backend-only for visual purposes. Arena names and rules text may differ, but the printed template does not identify them as a separate visual class.

These designations may still be used in development documents, filtering, deckbuilding tools, internal validation, and other non-card contexts.

---

## 13. Current open production questions

The following remain to be standardized or produced:

- exact primary, dark, light, and print-safe values for each faction color;
- exact ivory and parchment production values;
- final display serif, rules serif, and small-cap/sans-serif fonts;
- final vector construction for all six faction emblems;
- final Gauntlet wordmark;
- precise border width, keyline weight, safe-area spacing, and print tolerances;
- whether any faction-specific border ornament is needed after complete prototypes are tested;
- the exact Asset art-to-rules proportion and persistent-in-play cue;
- accessibility validation for color-blind recognition and low-ink printing;
- illustration crop and focal-point specifications for production assets;
- how physical and digital implementations adapt this shared language.

These questions should continue to be resolved incrementally and recorded here only after decisions are approved.
