# Gauntlet v0.6 Leader Archetype and Visual Notes

**Status:** Archived early art-direction / character-design notes.  
**Scope:** Historical faction-leader archetypes, visual inspiration, and design cautions. Current production direction lives in `docs/Gauntlet_v0.6_Leader_Design_Bible.md`.

---

## Overall direction

- Leader archetypes should help shape character design, silhouette, pose, costume, and faction identity.
- Keep the visual language roughly Colonial-era / early-modern-adjacent, but do not make Gauntlet a direct fantasy version of real-world history.
- Historical and fictional references are inspiration only, not literal depictions.
- Avoid overloading the setting with exact real-world analogues.
- Avoid culturally insensitive or overly literal use of sacred, living, or culturally specific traditions.

## Leader archetype notes

### Military — General

Current archetype references:

- George Washington
- Napoleon Bonaparte

Visual notes:

- Should read as battlefield command, grand strategy, and forward pressure.
- Should be mounted on a white horse.
- Strong candidate for a heroic or iconic mounted silhouette.

Open questions:

- Decide whether General leans more republican/Cincinnatus-style command, imperial/Napoleonic command, or a synthesis.

---

### Military — Commandant

Current archetype references:

- Not yet chosen.

Design direction:

- Should contrast with the General.
- General = forward offensive pressure.
- Commandant = garrison command, defensive authority, discipline, occupation, and counterattack.

Possible archetype space:

- Fortress commander.
- Drillmaster.
- Siege defender.
- Colonial garrison officer.

---

### Diplomats — Ambassador

Current archetype references:

- Thomas Jefferson.

Design direction:

- Should read as negotiation, letters, treaties, enlightened diplomacy, and soft power.
- More polished and intellectual than battlefield-facing.

Open questions:

- Decide whether Ambassador should visually emphasize statesmanship, foreign mission, or treaty-broker role.

---

### Diplomats — Senator

Current archetype references:

- Not yet chosen.

Design direction:

- Should contrast with Ambassador.
- Ambassador = negotiation abroad / successful accepted Terms.
- Senator = institutional resilience, legitimacy, political machinery, preserving influence through sacrifice.

Possible archetype space:

- Roman republican statesman.
- Colonial assemblyman.
- Parliamentary tactician.

---

### Inquisition — Grand Inquisitor

Current archetype references:

- Tomás de Torquemada.
- William Bradford.

Design cautions:

- Avoid real-world religious symbols.
- Avoid making Inquisition only anti-Arcane.
- Do not use scales; reserve scales for a possible future Legal faction.

Visual notes:

- Current faction symbol direction: wide-brimmed hat with buckle.
- Should communicate institutional judgment, condemnation, suppression, and Purge.

---

### Inquisition — Witch Hunter

Current archetype references:

- Van Helsing, as a loose fictional reference.

Design direction:

- Should read as pursuit, retaliation, field investigation, and relentless counterattack.
- Should contrast with Grand Inquisitor's institutional judgment.

Visual notes:

- Wide-brimmed hat / hunter silhouette is useful.
- Avoid making the design too modern vampire-hunter unless intentionally stylized.

---

### Arcane — Alchemist

Current archetype references:

- Nicolas Flamel.

Design direction:

- Should read as transmutation, sacrifice, forbidden knowledge, and turning loss into fuel.
- Should contrast with Spirit Walker: laboratory / formula / material transformation rather than trance / ritual / spirit-contact.

---

### Arcane — Spirit Walker

Current archetype references / visual inspiration:

- Earlier notes mentioned Navajo skinwalker legends as a reference point.
- Later visual direction called for inspiration from Maria Franz's stage look when performing with Heilung.

Design cautions:

- Treat culturally specific living traditions with caution.
- Avoid a direct appropriation of Navajo or other sacred/culturally specific figures.
- Prefer a more invented Gauntlet-specific ritualist silhouette if possible.

Design direction:

- Should read as ritual protection, Graveyard contact, trance, sacrifice, and keeping a Rite alive.
- Should contrast with Alchemist's more material/scientific transformation.

---

### Financiers — Banker

Current archetype references:

- Alexander Hamilton.

Design direction:

- Should read as credit, leverage, ledgers, collateral, and financing acquisition.
- Could lean toward founder-of-financial-system energy rather than generic wealthy merchant.

---

### Financiers — Executive

Current archetype references:

- Not yet chosen.

Design direction:

- Should contrast with Banker.
- Banker = credit, collateral, financing, institutional money.
- Executive = acquisition, hostile takeover, corporate command, aggressive expansion.

Possible archetype space:

- Industrial magnate.
- Robber baron.
- Chartered-company governor.
- Ruthless commercial administrator.

---

### Intelligence — Ranger

Current archetype references:

- Robert Rogers.

Design direction:

- Should read as fieldcraft, terrain expertise, scouting, and operating in hostile ground.
- Should contrast with Spymaster's command-and-control / network-planning role.

---

### Intelligence — Spymaster

Earlier shorthand:

- Spy.

Current archetype references:

- Robert Townsend.

Design direction:

- Should read as covert networks, hidden messages, mission planning, and intelligence coordination.
- The current v0.6 rules use **Spymaster**, not Spy.

- Senator
- Executive

Need final decision on whether to use:

- Witch Hunter: Van Helsing as inspiration or a more era-consistent hunter/investigator archetype.

---

## Relationship to rules

These visual notes should not change leader mechanics by themselves.

If a visual archetype starts implying a different mechanical identity, record that as a proposed rules change in the v0.6 Open Questions file rather than silently altering the Working Rules.
