# 052. Valor

**Status:** Approved  
**Source:** `releases/v0.5.7/Gauntlet_v0.5.7_Canonical_Data.json`

## v0.5.7 source

**Cost:** 2

**Action:**

> Bank Valor as an Asset. Whenever you lose a battle, draw one card after retreating.

**Battle:**

> After the battle dice are rolled, if your total is lower than your opponent's, you may reroll your die. You must use the new result.

## v0.6 decision

- **Cost:** 2
- **Allegiance:** Neutral
- **Starter eligible:** Yes
- **Complexity:** Basic
- **Watchlist:** Multiple-copy stacking and repeated loss-trigger draws

### Action

> Bank Valor as an Asset. Whenever you lose a battle, after resolving any required retreat, draw one card.

### Battle

> After the battle dice are rolled, if your battle total is lower than your opponent's, you may reroll your battle die. You must use the new result.

## Assessment

### Neutral-pool impact

Valor is clean shared resilience. It helps a losing player recover without preventing the loss, and its Battle effect offers a final uncertain chance rather than guaranteed reversal. Neither function is faction-defining.

### Faction-system interaction

Valor can support any faction that falls behind on the board or in battle without directly accelerating a faction-specific alternate victory. Its Asset occupies bank capacity and therefore carries an opportunity cost in Asset-heavy decks.

### Rationale

- Keep both mechanics essentially unchanged.
- Use **after resolving any required retreat** so the draw still occurs when retreat is modified, prevented, or impossible.
- Keep cost 2 because the Asset can generate repeated recovery over a game, while the Battle effect is conditional and replaces rather than supplements the original die result.
- Keep starter eligibility because both effects are intuitive and reinforce basic battle flow.

## Follow-up

- Test whether multiple Valor Assets create excessive card flow for a player who repeatedly loses battles.
