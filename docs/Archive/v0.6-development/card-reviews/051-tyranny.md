# 051. Tyranny

**Status:** Approved  
**Source:** `releases/v0.5.7/Gauntlet_v0.5.7_Canonical_Data.json`

## v0.5.7 source

**Cost:** 4

**Action:**

> Bank Tyranny as an Asset. Once per battle, the first opposing Battle card that would add to your opponent's battle total or grant them advantage has those portions of its effect ignored.

**Battle:**

> During this battle, opposing Battle cards cannot add to your opponent's battle total or grant them advantage.

**Reminder:**

> Other portions of an affected card still resolve.

## v0.6 decision

- **Cost:** 4
- **Allegiance:** Inquisition
- **Starter eligible:** No
- **Complexity:** Advanced
- **Watchlist:** Repeatable suppression, opponent-controlled target selection, multi-part Battle cards, and negation timing

### Action

> Bank Tyranny as an Asset. In each battle, after Battle cards are revealed, your opponent chooses one of their Battle cards that would add to their battle total or grant them advantage. That card is negated.

### Battle

> During this battle, all opposing Battle cards that would add to your opponent's battle total or grant them advantage are negated.

## Assessment

### Neutral-pool impact

Tyranny is not a necessary shared answer. Neutral already includes broad Battle-card disruption and cancellation, while Tyranny's repeatable suppression is a more faction-defining form of control.

### Faction-system interaction

Tyranny fits the Inquisition's identity of suppression and denial. It does not merely reduce numerical bonuses; it condemns qualifying Battle cards outright. The Action still gives the opponent meaningful counterplay because they choose which qualifying card is negated.

### Rationale

- Replace the ambiguous phrase **the first opposing Battle card** because the rules do not establish a universal resolution order among simultaneously revealed Battle cards.
- Let the opponent choose the qualifying card, preserving the intended ability to sacrifice a smaller bonus to protect a stronger one.
- Use **negated** to make clear that the whole chosen card has no effect, rather than only its bonus or advantage clause.
- Keep cost 4 because the Asset can repeatedly suppress one qualifying opposing card in every battle, while the Battle effect can negate an entire opposing Battle package.

## Follow-up

- Ensure the general rules define **negated** consistently as a card having no effect.
- Test whether opponent-selected negation provides enough counterplay against repeatable Tyranny Assets.
