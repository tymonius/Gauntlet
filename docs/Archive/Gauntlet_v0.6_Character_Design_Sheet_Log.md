## Generation / Iteration Notes

### General

Accepted direction:

- Mounted on a white horse.
- Colonial/early-modern battlefield commander.
- Reads as offensive tempo and forward pressure.
- Distinct from the Commandant because the General is mobile, heroic, and attack-oriented.

### Commandant

Accepted direction:

- On foot, severe, planted, and institutional.
- Defensive/garrison authority rather than heroic charge.
- Baton/cane and fortified base communicate Entrench / Repel / Fortify.

### Ambassador

Accepted after correction:

- First useful correction: Diplomat sheets should not use Military-style `Orders at a Glance` framing.
- Should use diplomatic / Terms / Influence presentation language instead.
- Visual direction is personal diplomacy, warmth, and successful negotiation.

Watchlist:

- `Good Offices` is still a weak/placeholder ability name.

### Senator

Accepted direction:

- Formal, procedural, institutional.
- Raised-hand orator silhouette.
- Distinct from Ambassador by being more legal and chamber-bound rather than warm and personally diplomatic.

### Grand Inquisitor

Accepted after correction:

- Must use the quasi-Puritan wide-brim buckle-hat silhouette, but in black felt.
- Tall and thin.
- Long black robes.
- Creepy and unsettling without becoming monstrous or supernatural.
- Smoking thurible is the preferred prop.

Guardrails:

- No real-world denominational religious symbols.
- No Spanish/Catholic Inquisition literalism.
- No scales, since scales may belong to a future Legal faction.

### Witch Hunter

Accepted after correction:

- Same broad-hat family as Grand Inquisitor, but materially different.
- Hat should be worn brown leather, not black felt.
- Lantern is the strongest primary prop.
- Rugged, muddy, mobile, and suspicious.

Guardrails:

- Do not make him a generic fantasy monster hunter.
- Keep him field-investigator / pursuer, not institutional judge.

### Alchemist

Accepted after multiple corrections:

- No hat.
- Should not resemble Inquisition leaders.
- Should be lab-coat-y rather than regal.
- Jeweler's loupe is the preferred eyewear/detail.
- Staining should be subtle and believable, not butcher-like.

Guardrails:

- Avoid steampunk goggles.
- Avoid wizard robes.
- Avoid excessive blood-red staining.

### Spirit Walker

Accepted direction:

- Antlered headdress.
- Chalky white and dark red face/body paint.
- Ritual-performance energy adapted into Gauntlet's setting.
- Staff, smoke, layered natural materials, charms, and Rite-circle base.

Guardrails:

- Avoid direct appropriation of a specific living sacred tradition.
- Avoid generic shaman cliches.

### Banker

Accepted direction:

- Should probably have spectacles; spectacles are now a defining marker.
- Refined financier, not miser caricature.
- Ledger / bonds / promissory notes / strongbox / scales.
- Represents abstract credit and collateral.

### Executive

Accepted after correction:

- Initial pass looked too similar to Banker.
- Corrected direction: larger, heavyset, meaner, more physically imposing.
- Chartered-company / monopoly-director energy.
- Uses charter, cane, warehouse keys, crates, barrels, and trade-route props.

Guardrails:

- Avoid spectacles as defining marker.
- Avoid delicate ledger-scholar silhouette.

### Ranger

Accepted after multiple corrections:

- Final direction: Robert Rogers mixed with Lewis and Clark.
- Colonial scout / rifleman / expedition surveyor.
- Long rifle, field journal, map case, compass, powder horn, and practical frontier gear.

Guardrails:

- No bow.
- No quiver.
- No wide-brim Witch Hunter hat.
- No fantasy ranger cloak.
- No generic monster-hunter look.

### Spymaster

Accepted after correction:

- Black gloves are locked.
- Visible hand should hold a sealed dispatch / intelligence report.
- Other hand hidden behind back holding a dagger.
- Spyglass should be a desk/base prop rather than hand-held.
- Reads as network commander / handler rather than field scout or assassin.

Guardrails:

- Avoid Ranger-like field reconnaissance.
- Avoid making him a crouching fantasy rogue.
- Avoid military uniform as primary identity.
