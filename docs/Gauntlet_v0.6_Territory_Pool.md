# Gauntlet v0.6 Territory Pool

**Status:** Authoritative working exact-text list for the complete v0.6 Territory pool  
**Territory count:** 25 unique designs — 21 standard Territories and 4 Arenas  
**Scope:** Current names, complexity, watchlists, status, and approved player-facing Territory text.

This document consolidates the approved v0.6 Territory review. It supersedes older Territory wording in the v0.5.7 canonical data and the individual review files for player-facing exact text. The files under `territory-reviews/` remain the design-provenance source for the original wording, rationale, and approval history.

This is not yet canonical v0.6 release data. The remaining faction packages and cross-card blockers must be completed before canonical data is created.

## General Territory rules

- A face-up Territory's printed effect is active unless its text states otherwise.
- Control and occupation matter only when the Territory specifically refers to them.
- A face-down Territory has no active printed effect.
- If an Overlay or card effect suppresses a Territory's printed effect, all of that printed effect is inactive for the stated duration.
- Overlays, dormant lower Overlays, and Ruins follow the general rules in `Gauntlet_v0.6_Working_Rules.md`.
- Territory deck-construction legality is governed by the Working Rules and eventual canonical v0.6 data rather than this exact-text pool.

# Standard Territories

## 1. Quicksand

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** None

> If a player begins their Movement phase on Quicksand, they cannot voluntarily withdraw or move more than one space that turn. Forced movement is unaffected.

## 2. Poisonous Gas

**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Watchlist:** Graveyard pressure, low-deck edge cases, and destination-changing effects

> During a battle on Poisonous Gas, each player may play no more than one Battle card. A card played from battle draw goes to its owner's Graveyard instead of its normal destination. If a player does not play a card from battle draw, during battle cleanup they place one unplayed card from that draw in their Graveyard.

## 3. Difficult Terrain

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Extra-movement and extra-Action interactions

> When a player enters Difficult Terrain, their movement ends for the turn. A player who begins their turn there or enters it during their turn cannot play an Action card after movement that turn.

## 4. Garrison

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Effects that increase or reduce the initial battle draw

> When Garrison's controller defends it, they draw one additional card for their initial battle draw.

## 5. Disrupted Supply Lines

**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Watchlist:** Asset-heavy factions and active/inactive timing

> While a player occupies Disrupted Supply Lines, only one banked Asset they control can be active. They choose which one remains active; all their other banked Assets are inactive until they leave.

## 6. Ruined Storehouse

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Recurring access to strong cards and start-of-turn draw modifiers

> Once at the beginning of their turn, when a player on Ruined Storehouse would draw a card from their deck, they may draw the top card of their discard pile instead.

## 7. Field Hospital

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Graveyard denial and repeated recovery during prolonged fighting

> During battle cleanup after a battle on Field Hospital, its controller may place one card they played that would go to their Graveyard in their discard pile instead.

## 8. Exposed Flank

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Hand-commitment denial stacking

> When Exposed Flank's controller counterattacks an opponent occupying it, that opponent cannot commit a Battle card from hand.

## 9. High Ground

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Defensive advantage stacking

> The defender in a battle on High Ground gains advantage.

## 10. Fortified Pass

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Asset-heavy faction matchups and suppression stacking

> When Fortified Pass's controller defends it, the attacker's banked Assets are inactive during that battle.

## 11. Insurgency

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Asset-heavy faction matchups and occupation-window suppression

> While an opponent occupies Insurgency without controlling it, their banked Assets are inactive.

## 12. Watchtower

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Commitment timing and information-effect interactions

> When Watchtower's controller defends it, the attacker commits any Battle card from hand face up instead of face down.

## 13. Supply Depot

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Draw acceleration and start-of-turn draw modifiers

> At the beginning of their turn, a player occupying and controlling Supply Depot draws one additional card as part of their normal draw.

## 14. Old Battlefield

**Status:** Approved for retention pending playtest  
**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Narrow utility, Graveyard setup by Mystics, and deliberate deck thinning

> During battle cleanup after a battle on Old Battlefield, its controller may place one unplayed card from their battle draw in their Graveyard instead of their discard pile.

## 15. Refuge

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Repeatable withdrawal effects and card-draw loops

> After a player voluntarily withdraws onto Refuge, they draw one card.

## 16. Command Tent

**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Watchlist:** Action combinations and effects that grant additional Action plays

> If a player begins their turn occupying and controlling Command Tent, they may play one Action card before movement and one Action card after movement that turn. If Command Tent is face down, they may reveal it at the beginning of that turn without using either Action play.

## 17. Monastery

**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Watchlist:** Matchups involving Mystics and final Arcane-trait assignment

> While Monastery's controller occupies it, cards cannot leave either player's Graveyard. During battles on Monastery, cards with the Arcane trait have no effect.

## 18. Training Grounds

**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Watchlist:** Increased battle-draw amounts and low-deck reshuffling

> When Training Grounds's controller defends it, after drawing their initial battle draw but before choosing cards to play from it, they may discard that entire draw and draw the same number of replacement cards. If they do, the replacement cards become their battle draw.

## 19. King's Road

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Movement stacking and consecutive battle initiation

> A player who begins their turn on King's Road gains one additional movement during that turn's Movement phase.

## 20. Toll Bridge

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Hand denial and movement-bonus interactions

> To voluntarily advance from Toll Bridge, a player must discard one card from their hand. If they cannot, they cannot advance from it.

## 21. Smuggler's Pass

**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Watchlist:** Hand-limit evasion, protected storage, and control-change timing

> As an Action while occupying and controlling Smuggler's Pass, you may stash one card from your hand face down underneath it. The stashed card does not count toward your hand limit. While you occupy and control Smuggler's Pass, you may play the stashed card as though it were in your hand; it counts as a card played from hand. At the beginning of your turn, if you control Smuggler's Pass, you may return the stashed card to your hand. If you lose control of Smuggler's Pass, place it in its owner's discard pile. Only one card may be stashed here.

# Arenas

## 22. Arena: Spoils of War

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Battle-draw increases and repeatable winner advantage

> During battles on Spoils of War, Homeland Advantage does not apply. If battle totals are tied, reroll the battle dice. During battle cleanup, the winner may put one unplayed card from their battle draw into their hand instead of discarding it.

## 23. Arena: No Quarter

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Retreat stacking and board-edge interactions

> During battles on No Quarter, Homeland Advantage does not apply. If battle totals are tied, reroll the battle dice. The loser retreats one additional space, if able.

## 24. Arena: Single Combat

**Complexity:** Basic  
**Watchlist:** Asset-reliant strategies and effects resolving immediately before battle

> During battles on Single Combat, Homeland Advantage does not apply. If battle totals are tied, reroll the battle dice. All banked Assets are inactive during the battle.

## 25. Arena: Grand Melee

**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Watchlist:** Multiple Battle-effect interactions and effects that modify battle-draw size or battle-drawn play allowances

> During battles on Grand Melee, Homeland Advantage does not apply. If battle totals are tied, reroll the battle dice. Each player draws one additional card for their initial battle draw and may play one additional card from that draw.

# Consolidated playtest priorities

- Determine whether Old Battlefield provides enough value outside dedicated Graveyard strategies.
- Test Grand Melee with cards and faction effects that increase battle-draw size or battle-drawn play allowances.
- Evaluate Monastery's pressure on the completed Mystics faction and confirm final Arcane-trait assignments.
- Verify Arena wording and behavior against final tie, Homeland Advantage, battle-draw, and battle-cleanup rules during canonical-data QA.
