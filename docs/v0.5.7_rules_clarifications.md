# Gauntlet v0.5.7 Rules Clarifications

This document records rules clarifications agreed upon during digital implementation and will be incorporated into the next formal rulebook release.

## Homeland Advantage

A defender has **Homeland Advantage** while defending any space they currently control.

- If battle totals are tied while the defender has Homeland Advantage, the defender wins the battle.
- Homeland Advantage applies whether the space is a Territory or a Heartland.

## Heartland Defense

Heartland defense is separate from Homeland Advantage.

- While defending your own Heartland, you gain **+1 to your battle total**.
- This bonus stacks with Homeland Advantage.

## Tied Battles

- If battle totals are tied and the defender has Homeland Advantage, the defender wins.
- Otherwise, the battle is tied and both players reroll their battle dice.
- Cards already played remain in effect during rerolls.

## Drawing Cards and Reshuffling

When drawing cards:

1. Draw as many cards as possible from your deck.
2. If additional cards are needed and your discard pile contains cards, shuffle the discard pile to form a new deck.
3. Continue drawing until the requested number of cards has been drawn or no cards remain available.
4. Cards already drawn during that draw action are never shuffled back into the deck.
5. The Graveyard is never reshuffled.

## Partial Draws

If a player cannot draw the full requested number of cards from their deck and discard pile combined, they draw as many cards as possible.

## Battle Draws

The normal battle draw is three cards.

However, card effects may modify:

- the number of cards drawn during battle, and/or
- the number of battle-drawn cards that may be played.

Rules text should refer to a player's **battle-draw amount** rather than assuming a fixed value of three.

## Battle-Draw Plays

A player may always choose to play fewer battle-drawn cards than they are allowed to play, including zero.

Battle-drawn cards are not hand commitments.

## Passing During Battle

- During the hand commitment step, a player may always choose to pass instead of committing a card from hand.
- During the battle-draw play step, a player may always choose to play fewer battle-drawn cards than allowed, including zero.

## Conscription

When Conscription is committed from hand before the battle draw step:

- increase that player's battle-draw amount by 1, and
- increase the number of battle-drawn cards they may play by 1.

## Watchtower

When attacking a revealed Watchtower controlled by the defender:

- the attacker's hand commitment is placed face up and is visible to both players;
- only the normal hand commitment is revealed this way;
- battle-drawn cards remain face down until the normal reveal step.

## Sanctions: Embargo

When resolving Sanctions: Embargo:

- if the opposing player has one or more active Battle cards, choose exactly one of those cards;
- the chosen card has no effect;
- if there are no opposing Battle cards, Sanctions: Embargo has no valid target and has no effect.

## Canceled Cards

When a played card is canceled:

- a canceled hand-committed card returns to its owner's hand;
- a canceled battle-drawn card is placed in the discard pile.
