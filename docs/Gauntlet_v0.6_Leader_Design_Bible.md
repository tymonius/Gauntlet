# Gauntlet v0.6 Leader Design Bible

**Status:** Working production-facing art and miniature direction for the v0.6 faction leader roster  
**Purpose:** Define the visual identity, tabletop miniature silhouette, and player-facing character direction for all twelve v0.6 faction leaders.

This document records the current character design direction for the twelve v0.6 faction leaders. It should be used alongside `docs/Gauntlet_v0.6_Working_Rules.md`, which remains the source of truth for rules text and leader abilities.

---

## 1. Purpose

Each faction leader should work as:

1. a full character illustration;
2. a player-facing leader card or reference sheet; and
3. a 3D tabletop miniature that moves across the board.

The leaders should feel like they belong to the same early-modern / Colonial-adjacent world while remaining visually distinct at tabletop scale.

---

## 2. Global Art Direction

### Setting Feel

**Powder, parchment, frontier, court, occult science, commerce, and covert operations.**

The world should feel roughly early-modern to Colonial-era, but not locked to a literal historical nation. Avoid pure fantasy medieval styling, modern industrial styling, or steampunk excess.

### Shared Visual Language

Use:

- wool, linen, leather, brass, iron, parchment, wax, smoke, wood, and stone;
- frock coats, waistcoats, robes, hunting frocks, cravats, buckled shoes, boots, and gaiters;
- muskets, sabers, maps, ledgers, sealed documents, field tools, ritual objects, and laboratory tools;
- muted natural colors with selective faction accents.

Avoid:

- medieval plate armor;
- modern suits;
- high fantasy wizard robes;
- overt steampunk gears or goggles;
- sci-fi military gear;
- real-world denominational religious symbols.

### Miniature Design Principles

Each miniature must have:

- a clear silhouette from across the table;
- one or two dominant props;
- a stable base;
- readable faction identity;
- a pose that reflects the leader ability;
- enough distinction from its faction counterpart.

The miniature should be more detailed than a chess piece, but still readable like a chess piece.

---

## 3. Faction Color and Material Notes

These are not strict faction colors, but production tendencies.

| Faction | Visual Materials / Mood |
|---|---|
| Military | Blue, brass, leather, smoke, field earth, polished weapons |
| Diplomats | Navy, ivory, parchment, wax seals, laurel, polished wood |
| Inquisition | Black, charcoal, brown leather, smoke, ash, iron, candlelight |

| Financiers | Dark green, burgundy, navy, brass, parchment, ledgers, wax |
| Intelligence | Muted green, ink black, parchment, walnut wood, brass, coded papers |

# Military Leaders

## 4. General

**Faction:** Military  
**Mechanical anchor:** Attack, forward pressure, tempo. Orders: Onward, Rally, Rout.  
**Core identity:** Battlefield momentum, aggressive command, visible leadership.

### Design Thesis

The General is the visible point of battlefield momentum. He leads from the front, inspires movement, and turns victory into further advance.

**Core phrase:** Forward. Again.

### Primary Visual Read

Mounted commander on a white horse.

### Character Design

The General should be a Colonial-era battlefield commander with elevated, heroic presence. He should lean more Washington than Napoleon, but may borrow some Napoleonic drama in the pose.

Use:

- white or pale gray horse;
- dark blue military coat;
- crimson or red sash;
- brass buttons;
- buff waistcoat / breeches;
- black riding boots;
- tricorn hat;
- raised saber or command gesture.

Avoid:

- medieval knight styling;
- armor;
- tyrant imagery;
- excessive Napoleonic bicorne styling;
- dismounted pose.

### Miniature Direction

The General is the only mounted leader.

Pose:

- horse stepping forward;
- General upright in saddle;
- saber angled forward or upward;
- reins in opposite hand;
- stable, not overly rearing.

Base:

- churned battlefield earth;
- broken fence rail;
- cannon fragment;
- discarded musket;
- torn flag fragment.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- mounted figure;
- white horse;
- raised saber;
- sash / military coat.

### Distinction

The General is motion and inspiration. The Commandant is structure and enforcement.

---

## 5. Commandant

**Faction:** Military  
**Mechanical anchor:** Defense, counterattack, holding captured ground. Orders: Entrench, Repel, Fortify.  
**Core identity:** Discipline, garrison control, defensive command.

### Design Thesis

The Commandant is the iron center of the line. He turns ground into position, position into discipline, and discipline into victory.

**Core phrase:** We hold. They break.

### Primary Visual Read

Rigid fortress officer standing on fortified ground.

### Character Design

The Commandant should feel institutional, severe, and military. He should not feel heroic in the same way as the General.

Use:

- immaculate military coat;
- epaulettes;
- tricorn;
- command baton, cane, or rod;
- sheathed saber;
- polished boots;
- stern expression;
- fortress / garrison background.

Avoid:

- mounted pose;
- windblown heroic charge;
- ragged field scout look;
- excessive aristocratic softness.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- standing upright;
- feet planted;
- one hand on baton or cane;
- other hand at belt, sword, or behind back;
- severe and immovable.

Base:

- stonework;
- palisade posts;
- sandbags;
- cannonballs;
- fortress keys;
- drill-yard elements.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- rigid vertical stance;
- baton / cane;
- broad officer shoulders;
- fortified base.

### Distinction

The General advances. The Commandant holds.

# Diplomat Leaders

## 6. Ambassador

**Faction:** Diplomats  
**Mechanical anchor:** Successful negotiation and soft value. Current ability name: Good Offices.  
**Core identity:** Persuasion, soft influence, accepted Terms.

### Design Thesis

The Ambassador turns trust, concession, and personal diplomacy into strategic advantage. He opens doors others keep closed.

**Core phrase:** Words first. War last.

### Primary Visual Read

Open-handed diplomat offering Terms.

### Character Design

The Ambassador should feel warm, refined, persuasive, and approachable. He wins through relationships, tact, and accepted arrangements.

Use:

- fine colonial coat;
- open hand gesture;
- treaty paper or sealed letter;
- diplomatic satchel;
- cravat;
- subtle spectacles optional;
- calm, intelligent expression;
- table with scrolls, quill, wax seals.

Avoid:

- courtroom severity;
- military command posture;
- sinister manipulation;
- excessive wealth display.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- one hand extended;
- other hand holding treaty, dispatch case, or sealed paper;
- relaxed but intentional posture.

Base:

- diplomatic table edge;
- parchment scrolls;
- wax seals;
- laurel motif;
- polished floor or cobblestone.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- outstretched hand;
- treaty paper;
- elegant coat;
- diplomatic desk/base props.

### Distinction

The Ambassador creates agreement through personal contact. The Senator preserves power through institutions and procedure.

### Watchlist

`Good Offices` is a functional placeholder but is not considered a strong final ability name.

---

## 7. Senator

**Faction:** Diplomats  
**Mechanical anchor:** Political resilience and Influence preservation. Ability: Political Capital.  
**Core identity:** Legitimacy, procedural power, institutional resilience.

### Design Thesis

The Senator protects legitimacy by spending resources, reputation, and procedure. He makes influence endure.

**Core phrase:** Hold the line. Build it stronger.

### Primary Visual Read

Formal orator with legal papers.

### Character Design

The Senator should feel institutional, legalistic, and stern. He is less charming than the Ambassador and more procedural.

Use:

- dark formal coat;
- ivory cravat;
- powdered wig or tied-back hair;
- raised hand in debate;
- law paper, petition, or statute;
- quill;
- legislative chamber or columns;
- stern expression.

Avoid:

- warm diplomatic gesture;
- battlefield or military pose;
- sinister Inquisition severity;
- merchant-finance props.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- one hand raised in objection or declaration;
- other holding bill, statute, or petition;
- upright and formal.

Base:

- chamber floor;
- small lectern;
- sealed legal papers;
- law books;
- wax seal.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- raised hand;
- formal document;
- orator posture;
- chamber/legal base.

### Distinction

The Ambassador negotiates. The Senator legislates, preserves, and legitimizes.

# Inquisition Leaders

## 8. Grand Inquisitor

**Faction:** Inquisition  
**Mechanical anchor:** Institutional judgment, suppression, and Purge after victory. Ability: Final Judgment.  
**Core identity:** Condemnation, suppression, Purification.

### Design Thesis

The Grand Inquisitor is the tribunal made flesh. He does not hunt. He condemns.

**Core phrase:** We judge. We purge.

### Primary Visual Read

Tall, thin, unsettling black-robed figure with thurible.

### Character Design

The Grand Inquisitor should feel creepy and severe without becoming supernatural or inhuman.

Use:

- tall, thin frame;
- long black robes;
- quasi-Puritan wide-brim buckle hat;
- black felt hat, not leather;
- pale face;
- restrained, unsettling expression;
- smoking thurible;
- tribunal/candle/smoke atmosphere.

Avoid:

- mitres;
- crosses;
- Catholic/Spanish Inquisition direct iconography;
- fantasy necromancer styling;
- monstrous anatomy;
- armor.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- upright, still, judicial;
- thurible hanging from one hand;
- other hand hidden in sleeve or holding writ;
- very vertical silhouette.

Base:

- tribunal stone;
- ash;
- iron keys;
- broken seals;
- writs;
- smoke.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- black buckle hat;
- long robes;
- smoking thurible;
- tall vertical body.

### Distinction

The Grand Inquisitor judges from institutional authority. The Witch Hunter hunts in the field.

---

## 9. Witch Hunter

**Faction:** Inquisition  
**Mechanical anchor:** Pursuit, retaliation, and punishing failed attacks. Ability: Relentless Pursuit.  
**Core identity:** Pursuit, retaliation, exposure.

### Design Thesis

The Witch Hunter is the mobile arm of the Inquisition. He finds what hides and punishes what overreaches.

**Core phrase:** You ran. I followed.

### Primary Visual Read

Worn leather hunter with lantern.

### Character Design

The Witch Hunter should look rugged, suspicious, and field-worn. He shares a broad-hat language with the Grand Inquisitor, but his materials and posture are completely different.

Use:

- worn brown leather wide-brim buckle hat;
- long dark travel coat;
- lantern held forward;
- muddy boots;
- satchel of tools;
- pistol or short blade;
- weathered leather straps;
- rain, mud, roots, ash.

Avoid:

- black felt judicial look;
- tall ceremonial robes;
- clerical symbols;
- heroic fantasy monster hunter excess;
- ornate armor.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- forward-moving stride;
- lantern extended;
- one hand near weapon;
- coat moving with motion.

Base:

- roots;
- cobblestones;
- ash;
- broken writs;
- footprints;
- chain or trap detail.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- lantern;
- worn brown buckle hat;
- forward stride;
- travel coat.

### Distinction

The Grand Inquisitor condemns. The Witch Hunter pursues.

# Mystic Leaders

## 10. Alchemist

**Faction:** Mystics  
**Mechanical anchor:** Sacrifice, Transmutation, and turning cards sent from hand to Graveyard into fuel. Ability name TBD.  
**Core identity:** Experiment, transmutation, disciplined occult science.

### Design Thesis

The Alchemist is a practical occult scientist. He studies, experiments, and transforms.

**Core phrase:** Nothing is fixed. Everything can be transformed.

### Primary Visual Read

Lab-coated early-modern experimental scholar with loupe and flask.

### Character Design

The Alchemist should feel like a working laboratory figure, not a wizard, noble, butcher, or Inquisition figure.

Use:

- no hat;
- messy scholar hair;
- off-white or bone-colored lab coat;
- mild stains only;
- leather apron;
- rolled sleeves or gloves;
- jeweler's loupe;
- raised flask;
- brass apparatus;
- notebooks and formulae;
- vials, crucible, retorts.

Avoid:

- wide-brim hat;
- regal cloak;
- skull-heavy necromancer elements;
- aggressive blood-like stains;
- steampunk goggles;
- Inquisition colors/silhouette.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- one hand holding flask up to inspect;
- loupe visible at eye or hanging at chest;
- other hand near workbench/apparatus;
- practical, focused posture.

Base:

- crucible;
- retort;
- glassware;
- notebook;
- formula pages;
- tools;
- small smoke effect.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- no hat;
- lab coat;
- raised flask;
- jeweler's loupe;
- laboratory base.

### Distinction

The Alchemist experiments with matter. The Spirit Walker invokes unseen forces.

---

## 11. Spirit Walker

**Faction:** Mystics  
**Mechanical anchor:** Ritual protection, Graveyard contact, and preserving a begun Rite through sacrifice. Ability name TBD.  
**Core identity:** Ritual endurance, unseen communion, protective invocation.

### Design Thesis

The Spirit Walker stands between seen and unseen worlds, preserving sacred work through sacrifice and endurance.

**Core phrase:** The spirits remember what the living abandon.

### Primary Visual Read

Antlered ritual figure with chalky white and dark red paint.

### Character Design

The Spirit Walker should feel ritual, primal, and otherworldly, while remaining part of Gauntlet's early-modern world. The look is inspired by ritual performance aesthetics rather than a direct copy of any specific culture.

Use:

- antlered headdress;
- chalky white face/body paint;
- dark red markings;
- layered natural garments;
- fur or wool mantle;
- bone and wood charms;
- ritual staff;
- smoke bowl or ritual vessel;
- wrapped boots;
- loose or braided hair.

Avoid:

- laboratory tools;
- wizard robes;
- Inquisition hats;
- direct living-culture sacred identifiers;
- generic tribal shaman cliches;
- pentagram-dominant imagery.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- tall, vertical, ceremonial;
- staff held upright;
- other hand holding smoke bowl or raised in invocation;
- protective posture near Rite circle.

Base:

- chalk Rite circle;
- carved stones;
- bound-card markers;
- ash;
- smoke;
- small ritual stakes.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- antlers;
- staff;
- layered mantle;
- face/body paint;
- ritual circle base.

### Distinction

The Alchemist transforms through experiment. The Spirit Walker preserves through invocation.

# Financier Leaders

## 12. Banker

**Faction:** Financiers  
**Mechanical anchor:** Collateral, leverage, and financing Deed purchases. Ability: Line of Credit.  
**Core identity:** Leverage, collateral, paper power, abstract finance.

### Design Thesis

The Banker turns promises into power. His strength is not wealth itself, but the ability to make paper, credit, and obligation move the world.

**Core phrase:** Credit is command before command is visible.

### Primary Visual Read

Refined colonial financier with spectacles and ledger.

### Character Design

The Banker should look intelligent, restrained, and financially powerful — not cartoonishly greedy.

Use:

- spectacles;
- dark green or navy coat;
- ivory cravat;
- ledger;
- promissory notes;
- coin scales;
- strongbox;
- quill;
- wax seals;
- bonds;
- counting-house setting.

Avoid:

- miser caricature;
- excessive coin-hoarding pose;
- mean heavy monopoly-boss silhouette;
- operational warehouse imagery, which belongs more to Executive.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- upright and composed;
- ledger or contract held in one arm;
- quill or coin in other hand;
- spectacles clearly visible.

Base:

- strongbox;
- coin scales;
- ledgers;
- sealed bonds;
- promissory notes.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- spectacles;
- ledger;
- refined coat;
- strongbox/scales base.

### Distinction

The Banker controls abstract value. The Executive controls physical systems and assets.

## 13. Executive

**Faction:** Financiers  
**Mechanical anchor:** Acquisition, hostile expansion, and immediate control after battlefield occupation. Ability: Hostile Takeover.  
**Core identity:** Acquisition, monopoly, logistics, direct control.

### Design Thesis

The Executive is the Company made flesh. He does not merely finance power; he seizes, owns, and administers it.

**Core phrase:** The Company owns what it governs.

### Primary Visual Read

Large, heavyset, mean chartered-company director.

### Character Design

The Executive must be visually distinct from the Banker. He should feel heavier, harsher, more domineering, and more operational.

Use:

- large/heavyset body type;
- mean expression;
- dark burgundy / black / deep navy coat;
- ornate but severe waistcoat;
- company charter;
- cane;
- warehouse keys;
- signet ring;
- crates, barrels, trade goods;
- manifests and route maps;
- chartered-company atmosphere.

Avoid:

- spectacles as defining feature;
- delicate ledger-scholar look;
- coin-counting pose;
- excessive military uniform;
- heroic nobility.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- broad stance;
- one hand holding charter or deed;
- other on cane or keys;
- heavy, commanding presence.

Base:

- crates;
- barrels;
- warehouse planks;
- trade goods;
- wax seals;
- keys;
- rolled route map.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- heavy frame;
- charter/deed;
- cane;
- crates/warehouse base.

### Distinction

The Banker lends and leverages. The Executive acquires and controls.

# Intelligence Leaders

## 14. Ranger

**Faction:** Intelligence  
**Mechanical anchor:** Fieldcraft, terrain expertise, hostile-ground operation. Ability: Fieldcraft.  
**Core identity:** Terrain mastery, reconnaissance, hostile-ground operation.

### Design Thesis

The Ranger is a frontier reconnaissance leader: Robert Rogers mixed with Lewis and Clark. He reads terrain, maps routes, and turns hostile ground into advantage.

**Core phrase:** Know the land before the battle begins.

### Primary Visual Read

Colonial expedition scout with long rifle and survey gear.

### Character Design

The Ranger should not look like a fantasy ranger or Witch Hunter. He is a grounded frontier scout, rifleman, and expedition surveyor.

Use:

- simple expedition cap;
- green-gray hunting frock;
- long flintlock rifle;
- powder horn;
- shot pouch;
- map case;
- field journal;
- compass / survey compass;
- compact spyglass;
- hatchet;
- wrapped boots or gaiters;
- clean, practical frontier equipment.

Avoid:

- bow;
- quiver;
- wide-brim buckle hat;
- long dark cloak;
- ragged fantasy ranger layers;
- monster-hunter mood;
- mystical wilderness look.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- one foot on uneven terrain;
- rifle held low and ready;
- body turned as if reading the route;
- mobile but controlled.

Base:

- roots;
- rocks;
- survey stake;
- rolled map;
- compass;
- revealed Territory marker;
- trail marker.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- long rifle;
- expedition cap;
- powder horn;
- low terrain posture;
- field tools on base.

### Distinction

The Ranger operates through terrain and field movement. The Spymaster operates through agents and networks.

## 15. Spymaster

**Faction:** Intelligence  
**Mechanical anchor:** Mission tempo, network coordination, and starting new Missions after completing one. Ability: Mission Control.  
**Core identity:** Mission tempo, network command, covert coordination.

### Design Thesis

The Spymaster is the architect of covert operations. He does not scout the land; he coordinates the people who do.

Composed intelligence chief with black gloves, dispatch, and hidden dagger.

- refined waistcoat;

- dagger hidden behind back;
- spyglass on desk/base;
- cipher wheel;
- map wall;
- pins and thread;
- coded letters;
- dead-drop pouch;
- lockbox;
- wax seals.

Avoid:

- field scout gear;
- wilderness pose;
- visible assassin crouch;
- fantasy rogue outfit;
- Ranger-like spyglass-in-hand field reconnaissance;
- military uniform.

### Miniature Direction

Pose:

- upright, composed;
- one hand holding sealed dispatch;
- other hand behind back holding dagger;
- calm face;
- controlled posture.

Base:

- desk edge;
- map papers;
- spyglass;
- cipher wheel;
- lockbox;
- sealed reports;
- pins/thread map.

### Key Silhouette Markers

- black gloves;
- visible dispatch;
- hidden dagger behind back;
- desk/map base;
- refined civilian posture.

### Distinction

The Ranger sees the field. The Spymaster moves the network.

## 20. Player-Facing Tone

The written descriptions should be short, evocative, and functional. Avoid lore overload. Each should make the player understand how the leader wins.

Good structure:

1. what the leader does;
2. how that differs from others;
3. one evocative closing line.

Example style:

> The Ranger reads the land before armies know they are lost. Where others see forest, river, and stone, he sees routes, risks, and advantage.
