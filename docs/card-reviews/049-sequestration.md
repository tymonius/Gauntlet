# 049. Sequestration

**Status:** Approved  
**Source:** `releases/v0.5.7/Gauntlet_v0.5.7_Canonical_Data.json` under the original title **The Black Edict**.

## v0.5.7 source

**Original title:** The Black Edict  
**Cost:** 4

**Action:**

> Each player chooses one Asset they control to keep and discards the rest.

**Battle:**

> All Assets are inactive during this battle.

## v0.6 decision

- **Title:** Sequestration
- **Cost:** 4
- **Allegiance:** Neutral
- **Starter eligible:** No
- **Complexity:** Advanced
- **Watchlist:** Mass Asset resets, comeback strength, and interaction with Asset-heavy faction engines

### Action

> Each player chooses one banked Asset they control to keep, if able, and discards the rest.

### Battle

> All banked Assets are inactive during this battle.

## Assessment

### Neutral-pool impact

Sequestration is important shared anti-snowball counterplay. Because Asset-bank capacity scales with controlled Territories, a player who is ahead on the board may also maintain a larger persistent engine. A symmetrical keep-one reset gives every faction access to a broad answer without guaranteeing one-sided value.

### Faction-system interaction

The effect should not be locked to Inquisition. Inquisition already has targeted, one-sided, Graveyard-based Asset punishment through Purge. Sequestration instead creates a symmetrical discard reset and temporary battle-wide suppression, making it a distinct neutral safety valve rather than faction machinery.

### Rationale

- Keep the mechanics essentially unchanged.
- Use **banked Asset** terminology for precision.
- Keep cost 4 because the Action can collapse developed board engines and the Battle effect can nullify substantial persistent investment.
- Exclude from starter decks because evaluating the timing and asymmetry of a mass reset requires familiarity with Asset-bank play.
- Rename the card because **The Black Edict** is too strongly coded to the Inquisition and should be reserved for a purpose-built Inquisition card.

## Reserved title

**The Black Edict** is reserved for a future Inquisition card. Its eventual mechanics should reinforce Conviction, Condemnation, Purge, or Purification rather than inherit Sequestration's neutral reset effects by default.

## Follow-up

- Design the future Inquisition card **The Black Edict** during the Inquisition faction-card pass.
- Test whether Sequestration produces healthy comeback pressure without erasing too much long-term planning.
