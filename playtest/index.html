Gauntlet v0.6.0 Playtest Packet

Use with the canonical v0.6.0 rulebook and, for first games, the recommended Deck for each selected Leader.

Facilitator rule: Record the problem before proposing a fix. Do not change a rule or card during the game unless the test was explicitly scheduled as a variant comparison.

Before play

  1. Record the exact version, Decks, Leaders, Territory order, first player, and any intentional variant.
  2. For onboarding tests, let players use the rulebook and player aids without strategic coaching.
  3. Explain that honest criticism is more useful than politeness and that feedback may be used in later versions.
  4. Choose the test mode below. A single game should answer a small number of primary questions.

During play

  1. Note rule lookups, disagreements, difficult physical states, and moments when play stops flowing.
  2. Count objective events where practical, but do not interrupt players for perfect telemetry.
  3. Do not rescue players from strategic mistakes in a blind or onboarding test.
  4. Mark the moment the result first seemed inevitable and whether meaningful recovery decisions remained.

After play

  1. Have each player complete their evaluation privately before discussing the game.
  2. Conduct the joint debrief, then classify issues by category and severity.
  3. Separate rules confusion, production friction, balance concerns, and personal preference.
  4. One unusual game is evidence, not a verdict. Deterministic exploits and impossible resolutions are exceptions.
