# Gauntlet v0.6 Diplomat Faction Guide

> **Definitive v0.6 Diplomat faction source.** This guide governs all Diplomat-specific rules, Leaders, Influence, Terms, Proposals, Treaty Articles, supplemental components, and the canonical twelve-card Diplomat pool. General movement, battle, capture, Asset, Overlay, and victory rules remain in the core rulebook.

# 1. Diplomat overview

The Diplomats use Influence and Terms to convert impending battles into settlements or to make refusal more costly. They may win by running the Gauntlet or by completing the Peace Treaty.

## At a glance

| **ELEMENT** | **DIPLOMAT RULE** |
|---|---|
| **Victory** | Run the Gauntlet or complete the Peace Treaty. |
| **Resource** | Influence, minimum 0 and maximum 10. |
| **Starting value** | Begin with 1 Influence. |
| **Faction action** | Before a battle involving you, offer Terms by choosing one eligible Proposal and staking its listed Influence. |
| **Leaders** | Ambassador and Senator. |
| **Faction pool** | 12 Diplomat card titles. |
| **Proposal set** | Nine double-sided Proposal / Treaty Article cards. |

# 2. Components and setup

## Components

A Diplomat Deck includes:

- one Diplomat Leader Card: **Ambassador** or **Senator**;
- one shared **Influence Tracker**;
- one double-sided **Diplomat Reference Card**;
- nine double-sided **Proposal / Treaty Article cards**; and
- any Diplomat cards included in the Playable Deck.

## Setup

1. Place the chosen Leader Card face up near your play area.
2. Place the Influence Tracker beneath it and align the Leader Card at 1 Influence.
3. Place all nine Proposal cards Proposal side up.
4. Keep the Diplomat Reference Card available.

The Leader Card is the Influence indicator; no separate Influence tokens are used.

# 3. Influence and Terms

## Influence

Diplomats begin with 1 Influence and may have up to 10.

Only the Influence currently shown on the tracker is available. When you stake Influence, lower the tracker by the listed amount. Staked Influence is unavailable until the Terms resolve. Influence gained above 10 is lost.

## Offering Terms

Before a battle involving you, before either player commits a card from hand, you may offer Terms.

1. Choose one eligible Proposal.
2. Confirm that its Requirement is satisfied.
3. Confirm that you have at least its listed Stake available.
4. Lower your available Influence by the Stake.
5. The opponent accepts or refuses the Terms.

You may normally offer only one Proposal for a battle.

## Accepted Terms

When the opponent accepts:

1. No battle occurs.
2. Resolve the Proposal's **Accepted** effect.
3. Return the staked Influence.
4. If the Proposal is not already ratified, flip it to its Treaty Article side.
5. If it was newly ratified, gain Influence equal to its Stake.
6. Resolve any ability or card effect that occurs after acceptance.

A Proposal with a Stake of 0 grants no Influence merely for being accepted.

## Refused Terms

When the opponent refuses:

1. Resolve the Proposal's **Refused** effect and any effect modifying the refusal.
2. Continue into the battle normally.
3. Before dice are rolled, you may use Leverage.
4. Resolve the battle.

Then apply the result:

| **RESULT** | **STAKE** | **RATIFICATION** | **NORMAL REWARD** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **You win** | Return it | If new, impose and flip the Proposal | Gain 1 Influence unless stated otherwise |
| **You lose** | Lose it | Do not ratify | None |
| **No winner** | Return it | Do not ratify | None |

To **impose** a Proposal is to ratify it after winning the battle caused by its refusal.

## Leverage

Before dice are rolled in a battle following refused Terms, you may spend any amount of available Influence. Add +1 to your battle total for each Influence spent.

Staked Influence is not available for Leverage.

## Ratified Proposals

A ratified Proposal is a **Treaty Article**.

A Treaty Article may be offered again for its tactical effects, but it cannot be ratified again, does not count as an additional Article, and grants no normal newly-ratified Influence reward.

## Peace Treaty

At the start of your turn, after the Capture step and before the Draw step, if five different Proposals are ratified, you win the game.

Ratifying the fifth Proposal does not end the game immediately. The Peace Treaty is checked only at the stated timing.

# 4. Diplomat Leaders

## Ambassador

<img src="../../../../images/sketches/ambassador.png" alt="Ambassador" />

**Archetype:** Agreement, card flow, and settlement  
**Motto:** *Words first. War last.*

The Ambassador converts accepted Terms into additional cards, sustaining further negotiations and defenses.

### Cordiality

Once per turn, after the opponent accepts your Terms, draw one card.

## Senator

<img src="../../../../images/sketches/senator.png" alt="Senator" />

**Archetype:** Risk management, resilience, and imposition  
**Motto:** *Procedure endures.*

The Senator can preserve staked Influence after losing a battle by permanently sacrificing cards from hand.

### Political Capital

Once per turn, when you would lose staked Influence after losing a battle following refused Terms, you may put up to that many cards from your hand in your Graveyard. Recover 1 staked Influence for each card put there; lose the rest.

# 5. Diplomat-specific rules

## Terms timing

The Terms offer occurs after the movement or effect that would begin the battle but before either player commits a card from hand.

If Terms are accepted, the battle never begins. If Terms are refused, proceed with the battle after resolving all refusal effects.

## Multiple Proposals

Diplomatic Latitude may allow two eligible Proposals with the same Stake to be offered together. Stake that amount only once. Only the selected Proposal resolves or may become ratified.

## No-winner battles

If a battle following refused Terms ends without a winner, return the Stake and do not ratify the Proposal. Safe Conduct can create this result.

## Sanctions

**Sanctions** is a shared title used by three Diplomat cards. It is not a separate card type.

Each Sanction identifies the opponent who refused the Terms that allowed it to enter play. Its ongoing effect and relief condition apply to that opponent.

## Proposal set

The following nine Proposal cards remain available throughout the game. When a Proposal is ratified, flip it to its Treaty Article side. Its Requirement, Accepted effect, Refused effect, and Stake remain available when offered again.

### De-escalation

**Stake:** 0  
**Requirement:** None

> **Accepted:** Both players withdraw. The opponent draws one card.
>
> **Refused:** Draw one card.

### Orderly Withdrawal

**Stake:** 0  
**Requirement:** You must be attacking.

> **Accepted:** You withdraw. The opponent remains in or occupies the contested position, then draws one card.
>
> **Refused:** Add +1 to your battle total.

### Capitulation

**Stake:** 0  
**Requirement:** You must be defending.

> **Accepted:** You withdraw. The opponent remains in or occupies the contested position, then draws one card.
>
> **Refused:** If you lose, draw two cards.

### Open Channels

**Stake:** 1  
**Requirement:** You must have a card in hand.

> **Accepted:** Both players reveal their hands, then withdraw. The opponent draws one card.
>
> **Refused:** Look at the opponent's hand. When forming your Battle Hand, draw one additional card before choosing a card from it.

### Mutual Disarmament

**Stake:** 1  
**Requirement:** Both players must have a card in hand.

> **Accepted:** Each player discards one card from hand. The opponent draws one card, then both players withdraw.
>
> **Refused:** You may discard one card from hand. If you do, when forming your Battle Hand, draw one additional card before choosing a card from it.

### Prisoner Exchange

**Stake:** 1  
**Requirement:** Each player must have a card in their Graveyard.

> **Accepted:** Each player may move one card from their Graveyard to their Discard Pile. Then both players withdraw.
>
> **Refused:** If you lose, you may move one card from your Graveyard to your Discard Pile.

### Rebuilding Pact

**Stake:** 1  
**Requirement:** You must have a card in hand that can be banked as an Asset.

> **Accepted:** Each player may bank one eligible card from hand as an Asset without using an Action Opportunity. Then both players withdraw.
>
> **Refused:** After the battle, you may bank one eligible card from hand as an Asset without using an Action Opportunity.

### Ultimatum

**Stake:** 2  
**Requirement:** None

> **Accepted:** The opponent withdraws. You remain in or occupy the contested position.
>
> **Refused:** Add +1 to your battle total. If you win and newly ratify this Proposal, gain 2 Influence instead of 1.

### Diplomatic Recognition

**Stake:** 2  
**Requirement:** You must be defending a counterattack on a Territory you occupy that the opponent controlled immediately before you occupied it.

> **Accepted:** Capture that Territory immediately. The opponent withdraws, then draws two cards.
>
> **Refused:** If you win, capture that Territory immediately, but gain no Influence for imposing this Proposal.

# 6. Canonical card pool

The following entries are the canonical v0.6 Diplomat names, costs, card forms, and exact player-facing text.

## Clemency

**Cost:** 1

> **Action:** Choose a card in the opponent's Graveyard. They choose one:
>
> - Move it to their Discard Pile; you gain 1 Influence.
> - Leave it there; you draw one card.

## Trade Concessions

**Cost:** 2

> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may reveal this from hand and set it aside.
>
> **Accepted:** The opponent chooses one available option:
>
> - Draw two cards.
> - Bank one eligible card from hand as an Asset without using an Action Opportunity.
>
> Then discard this and draw one card.
>
> **Refused:** Return this to your hand before the battle.
>
> **Battle:** The opponent draws one card. Add +2 to your battle total.

## Safe Conduct

**Cost:** 2  
**Card form:** Asset

> **Action:** Bank this as an Asset.
>
> When you would lose a battle following refused Terms, you may discard this to withdraw instead. The opponent remains in or occupies the contested position, and the battle ends without a winner.

## Neutral Observers

**Cost:** 2  
**Card form:** Asset

> **Action:** Bank this as an Asset.
>
> After Terms are refused, before either player commits a card from hand, you may discard this. The opponent first commits one card from hand face up for its Battle effect or declines to commit; then you commit normally.

## Good Faith

**Cost:** 3  
**Card form:** Asset

> **Action:** Bank this as an Asset.
>
> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may discard this to draw one card, then reveal one card from hand and set it aside.
>
> **Accepted:** Put that card in your Graveyard.
>
> **Refused:** Return it to your hand before the battle.

## Demilitarized Zone

**Cost:** 3  
**Card form:** Territory Overlay

> After your Terms are accepted and the Proposal's Accepted effect resolves, you may place this as an Overlay on the contested Territory without using an Action Opportunity. Each player still there withdraws.
>
> Neither player may enter this Territory during the turn this is placed.
>
> To enter it while unoccupied, discard one card.
>
> It cannot be captured or change control while this remains.
>
> At the start of your turn, before the Capture step, if you occupy it, discard one card or withdraw.
>
> After the first battle fought here, discard this.

## Diplomatic Latitude

**Cost:** 3

> When you offer Terms, you may reveal this from hand and offer two eligible Proposals with the same Stake instead of one. Stake that amount once.
>
> **Accepted:** The opponent chooses which Proposal is accepted.
>
> **Refused:** Choose one Proposal before resolving its Refused effect. Only it may be imposed.
>
> The other Proposal does not resolve or become ratified. After the Terms resolve, discard this.

## Nonbinding Resolution

**Cost:** 3

> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may reveal this from hand and set it aside.
>
> **Accepted:** If the Proposal is unratified, the opponent chooses one before ratification:
>
> - Ratify it normally.
> - Leave it unratified; you gain 2 Influence.
>
> After the accepted Terms resolve, discard this, then draw one card.
>
> **Refused:** Discard this, then draw one card before the battle.

## Sanctions: Censure

**Cost:** 3  
**Card form:** Asset

> After an opponent refuses your Terms, you may bank this from hand without using an Action Opportunity.
>
> While banked, the first time each turn that opponent plays a card for its Action effect, they choose: discard one card, or you draw one card.
>
> After they accept your Terms, discard this.

## Gunboat Diplomacy

**Cost:** 4

> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may reveal this from hand.
>
> **Accepted:** Discard this.
>
> **Refused:** Reveal this face up in the resulting battle for its Battle effect.
>
> **Battle:** Add +2 to your battle total. After the battle, put this in your Discard Pile.

## Sanctions: Embargo

**Cost:** 4  
**Card form:** Asset

> After an opponent refuses your Terms, you may bank this from hand without using an Action Opportunity.
>
> While banked, that opponent's Asset limit is reduced by 1, to a minimum of 0.
>
> After they accept your Terms, discard this.

## Sanctions: Blockade

**Cost:** 5  
**Card form:** Territory Overlay

> After the battle following an opponent's refusal of your Terms, you may place this as an Overlay on a Territory they control without using an Action Opportunity.
>
> The first time each turn that opponent enters or leaves this Territory, they choose: discard one card, or you gain 1 Influence.
>
> Discard this after they accept your Terms or lose control of this Territory.

# Appendix A. Diplomat quick reference

## Influence

- Begin with 1 Influence.
- Minimum 0; maximum 10.
- Lower the tracker when staking or spending Influence.
- Staked Influence is unavailable until Terms resolve.
- Leverage spends available Influence for +1 battle total each.

## Terms

| **OUTCOME** | **STAKE** | **RATIFICATION** | **NORMAL REWARD** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Accepted** | Return | If new, flip to Treaty Article | Gain Influence equal to Stake |
| **Refused — win** | Return | If new, impose and flip | Gain 1 Influence unless stated otherwise |
| **Refused — loss** | Lose | No | None |
| **No winner** | Return | No | None |

## Leader abilities

| **LEADER** | **ABILITY** | **EFFECT** |
|---|---|---|
| **Ambassador** | Cordiality | Once per turn, after the opponent accepts your Terms, draw one card. |
| **Senator** | Political Capital | Once per turn, when a refused battle loss would lose staked Influence, put up to that many cards from hand in your Graveyard; recover 1 staked Influence per card. |

## Proposal list by Stake

| **STAKE** | **PROPOSALS** |
|---:|---|
| **0** | De-escalation, Orderly Withdrawal, Capitulation |
| **1** | Open Channels, Mutual Disarmament, Prisoner Exchange, Rebuilding Pact |
| **2** | Ultimatum, Diplomatic Recognition |

## Reminders

- Offer Terms before cards are committed from hand.
- Only available Influence can be used for Leverage.
- A Treaty Article may be offered again but cannot be ratified or rewarded again normally.
- Sanctions are a card-title series, not a separate card type.
- Check Peace Treaty after the Capture step at the start of your turn.
