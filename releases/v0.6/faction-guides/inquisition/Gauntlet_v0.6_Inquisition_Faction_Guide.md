## 1. Inquisition overview

The Inquisition turns the opponent's played cards, discarded resources, exposed hand, and battlefield losses into evidence. It can win normally by breaking through the opponent's Heartland or by permanently condemning enough of the opponent's deck that a normal start-of-turn draw becomes impossible.

> **Faction identity:** Turn the opponent's commitments into evidence, then decide whether to suppress the present threat or remove it permanently.

- **Victory:** normal breakthrough victory or Purification.
- **Resource:** Conviction, maximum 4.
- **Resource gain:** the first time each turn one or more opposing cards enter the Graveyard after a battle involving you, gain 1 Conviction, up to the maximum.
- **Leaders:** Grand Inquisitor or Witch Hunter.
- **Faction pool:** 12 unique cards; curve 1 / 3 / 4 / 2 / 2 at costs 1-5.
- **Cost-5 statements:** Heresy and Hellfire.
- **Arcane-trait card:** Heresy.

### Components and setup

An Inquisition deck uses:

1. one selected Leader Card: **Grand Inquisitor** or **Witch Hunter**;
2. one **Inquisition Doctrine** reference;
3. one **Purge Reference**;
4. one shared **Conviction Tracker** placed beneath the selected Leader Card;
5. any Inquisition playable cards included through normal deck construction.

At 0 Conviction, the Leader Card fully covers the tracker. Slide the Leader upward until its bottom edge reaches the 1, 2, 3, or 4 registration line as Conviction changes. No token or separate marker is used.

## 2. Conviction, doctrine, Purge, and Purification

### Conviction

> **Conviction:** Maximum 4. The first time each turn one or more opposing cards enter the Graveyard after a battle involving you, gain 1 Conviction.

The trigger is not limited to your own turn. An opposing hand commitment normally enters the Graveyard after battle, and Condemnation sends opposing played battle-drawn cards there as well. If several opposing cards enter the Graveyard during the same after-battle process, gain only 1 Conviction from the normal trigger.

Conviction above the maximum is lost. Spending Conviction is optional unless an effect says otherwise.

### Condemnation

In battles involving you, opposing played battle-drawn cards go to their owner's Graveyard after the battle instead of discard. Opposing hand commitments already go to the Graveyard. Unplayed battle-drawn cards are discarded normally.

### Blasphemy

Whenever the opponent plays a card with the **Arcane** trait, gain 1 Conviction. This gain is outside the normal once-per-turn limit but cannot exceed the maximum. The Arcane trait is separate from faction allegiance; not every Arcane card belongs to Mystics.

### Purge

Instead of playing an Action card during the Action step, spend Conviction to choose one eligible Purge:

| Cost | Effect |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Choose one: send the top card of the opponent's discard pile to their Graveyard; or choose up to two cards in their discard pile with combined deckbuilding value 2 or less and send them to their Graveyard. |
| 2 | Choose one opposing Asset and send it to its owner's Graveyard. |
| 3 | The opponent chooses one card from hand and sends it to their Graveyard. |
| 4 | Look at the opponent's hand. Choose one card and send it to their Graveyard. |

A Purge replaces the opportunity to play an Action card; it is not an Action card. Final Judgment is the only current rule that permits a Purge outside this normal opportunity.

### Purification

At the beginning of the opponent's turn, after their normal start-of-turn draw attempt, if they draw no cards because both their deck and discard pile are empty, you win by **Purification**.

Purification does not trigger from a failed battle draw or card-effect draw. The opponent may continue playing while cards remain in hand or other zones; the victory check occurs only after the normal start-of-turn draw attempt.

## 3. Inquisition leaders

## Grand Inquisitor

![Grand Inquisitor sketch](../../../../images/sketches/grand inquisitor.png)

**We judge. We purge.**  
**Style:** institutional judgment, post-victory punishment, efficient Purges.

The Grand Inquisitor is the tribunal made flesh. He turns a battlefield victory into an immediate judgment, using the moment of defeat to condemn an Asset, a card in hand, or part of the opponent's future deck before they can recover.

Choose the Grand Inquisitor when you want to combine conventional battle victories with destructive post-battle sequences and carefully ration Conviction for discounted Purges.

### Final Judgment

> **Once per turn, after you win a battle, you may immediately Purge without using the Action opportunity. Reduce that Purge's Conviction cost by 1, minimum 1.**

Grand Inquisitor priorities:

- preserve at least 1 Conviction before a likely victory so Final Judgment remains available;
- spend only as much Conviction as needed on Tyranny or Hellfire to secure the battle;
- compound a victory with Excommunication, Act of Faith, Burning at the Stake, or a discounted Purge;
- decide whether Divine Mercy's immediate Conviction is worth restoring a condemned card to discard.

## Witch Hunter

![Witch Hunter sketch](../../../../images/sketches/witch hunter.png)

**You ran. I followed.**  
**Style:** defense, retaliation, pursuit, exposure.

The Witch Hunter tracks corruption into the places it hides and punishes attackers who fail to finish what they began. He turns a successful defense into an abrupt end to the opponent's turn, then advances before they can reorganize.

Choose the Witch Hunter when you want to defend efficiently, deepen the cost of failed attacks, and reserve Conviction for immediate counterattack rather than spending everything on Purges.

### Relentless Pursuit

> **Once per turn, after an opponent loses a battle they initiated against you, you may spend 2 Conviction. If you do, end their turn, then move one space toward their Heartland. If this starts a battle, resolve it immediately; you are the attacker, and neither player may play an Action before it.**

Witch Hunter priorities:

- preserve 2 Conviction when a defensive victory could create a decisive counterattack;
- use Penance or Divine Mercy to reach the pursuit threshold;
- use No Martyrs to prevent a defeated attacker from profiting from loss or retreat;
- treat Purification pressure as a constraint on the opponent while advancing conventionally.

## 4. Playing Inquisition

The central Inquisition decision is **present control versus permanent judgment**.

After evidence accumulates, decide whether Conviction is best spent to negate a current Battle card, Purge a vulnerable target, enable a leader ability, appropriate an enemy effect through Heresy, or fuel Hellfire.

### Strengths

- turns opposing battle commitments into permanent loss through Condemnation;
- pressures the opponent's discard pile, hand, deck, Assets, and recovery plans;
- converts exposed information into coercive choices and selective destruction;
- threatens both normal breakthrough and literal deck-exhaustion victory;
- uses the same card pool differently under a post-victory judge and a retaliatory hunter.

### Limits

The Inquisition has:

- limited unconditional raw battle power;
- conditional and opponent-influenced Conviction generation;
- limited movement and no routine capture acceleration;
- a slow alternate victory vulnerable to large decks and Graveyard recovery;
- dependence on exposed cards, populated discard piles, and opponent commitments;
- no blanket ability to prevent recovery or control every opposing zone.

### Strategic threads

| Thread | Cards |
|---|---|
| Evidence and coercion | Accusation; Confession; Penance; Burning at the Stake |
| Permanent condemnation | Excommunication; Guilt by Association; Act of Faith; Hellfire |
| Present suppression | Tyranny; Confession; No Martyrs |
| Conviction conversion | Penance; Divine Mercy; Tyranny; Heresy; Hellfire |
| Forbidden appropriation | Heresy |
| Conventional punishment | No Martyrs; Burning at the Stake; Hellfire |

The pool is a strategic vocabulary, not a set of required packages. Large low-cost decks are legitimate resistance to Purification; when an opponent sacrifices too much card quality to resist exhaustion, pursue normal breakthrough instead.

### Playing against Inquisition

- commit from hand only when the Battle effect or tactical need justifies permanent loss;
- keep the discard pile's top card and duplicate titles in mind before presenting easy Purge or Guilt by Association targets;
- pressure Conviction timing by forcing choices between Purge, Tyranny, Hellfire, and leader abilities;
- use larger decks or Graveyard recovery to resist Purification, but do not concede too much immediate card quality;
- attack the Grand Inquisitor before a destructive Final Judgment sequence is prepared, and respect the Witch Hunter's 2-Conviction pursuit threshold.

## 5. Inquisition-specific rules reference

### Opposing card enters the Graveyard after battle

The normal Conviction trigger checks the after-battle destination process. One or several opposing cards entering the Graveyard from that battle produce only one normal Conviction gain for that turn.

### Battle-drawn card

A **battle-drawn card** is a card played from battle draw. Against the Inquisition, an opposing played battle-drawn card goes to the Graveyard after battle because of Condemnation. Unplayed battle-drawn cards still go to discard.

### Hand commitment

A Battle card deliberately committed from hand normally goes to the Graveyard after battle. Condemnation does not change that existing destination.

### Discard-pile order

The top card of a discard pile is the most recently placed card unless a rule explicitly changes the order. Effects that choose any card in discard do not reorder the remaining pile.

### Combined deckbuilding value

When an effect permits several cards with a combined value limit, add their printed deckbuilding values. The total cannot exceed the stated limit. A zero-card choice is legal only when the effect says "up to."

### Negate

A negated card remains played and follows its normal destination, but its text has no effect unless another rule changes that destination. Tyranny does not cancel the opponent's play or return the card to its source.

### Beneficial loss and retreat triggers

No Martyrs prevents the losing opponent from benefiting from effects they control that trigger from that loss or resulting retreat. It does not prevent ordinary retreat, harmful consequences, or effects controlled by another player.

### Copied Battle effects and Heresy

Normally, an effect that resolves another Battle effect cannot select another copying effect. Heresy permits one additional copied-effect layer: Heresy may resolve an opposing Battle effect that itself resolves one additional Battle effect, but that additional effect cannot resolve another.

The chosen Graveyard card remains where it is. Resolve only effect text that can legally function at the current timing; source-dependent or impossible instructions are handled by the core copied-effect rules.

### Purification timing

Purification is checked only after the opponent's normal start-of-turn draw attempt. Cards remaining in hand, Graveyard, the Asset Bank, or other zones do not refill the deck or discard unless a rule moves them there.

## 6. Canonical Inquisition card pool

### Accusation

**Cost:** 1  
**Complexity:** Basic

> **Action:** Choose a card in the opponent's discard pile. They put it on top of their deck or send it to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose a card in the opponent's discard pile. They put it on top of their deck or send it to their Graveyard.

### Confession

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> **Action:** Look at the opponent's hand and choose a Battle card. Until end of turn, if they commit from hand in a battle involving you, they must commit that card if able.
>
> **Battle:** After both players select their Battle cards, before reveal, reveal Confession and the opponent's hand commitment, if any. You may return your hand commitment to your hand and replace it with another Battle card from hand, played face up.

Confession permits one informed replacement, not an additional commitment or Battle-card play.

### Penance

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Basic

> **Action:** The opponent chooses one: send a card from hand to their Graveyard; or you gain 1 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** After Battle cards are revealed, the opponent chooses one: send a card from hand to their Graveyard; or add +1 to your battle total.

### Divine Mercy

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Basic

> **Action:** Move a card from the opponent's Graveyard to their discard pile. Gain 2 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** Move a card from the opponent's Graveyard to their discard pile. Add +2 to your battle total.

Divine Mercy deliberately reverses one permanent loss in exchange for immediate power.

### No Martyrs

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> **Action:** Bank No Martyrs as an Asset. After Battle cards are revealed in a battle involving you, you may discard it. If you do and the opponent loses, they cannot benefit from effects they control triggered by that loss or retreat, and retreat one additional space.
>
> **Battle:** If the opponent loses, they cannot benefit from effects they control triggered by that loss or retreat, and retreat one additional space.

### Excommunication

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> **Action:** Choose one or more cards in the opponent's discard pile with combined deckbuilding value up to 5. Send them to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose one or more cards in the opponent's discard pile with combined deckbuilding value up to 3. Send them to their Graveyard.

### Guilt by Association

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Basic

> **Action:** Choose a card in the opponent's discard pile. Send every card there with that title to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, choose a card the opponent played. Send every card in their discard pile with that title to their Graveyard.

### Act of Faith

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Basic

> **Action:** Reveal up to three cards from the top of the opponent's deck. Send one to their Graveyard and discard the rest.
>
> **Battle:** After the battle, reveal up to two cards from the top of the opponent's deck. Send one to their Graveyard and discard the rest.

If only one card is revealed, send it to the Graveyard.

### Tyranny

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> **Action:** Bank Tyranny as an Asset. Once per turn, after Battle cards are revealed in a battle involving you, you may spend 1 Conviction to negate one opposing played Battle card.
>
> **Battle:** Negate one opposing played Battle card.

### Burning at the Stake

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> **Action:** The opponent reveals their hand. Send the card with the highest deckbuilding value to their Graveyard; choose among ties. If it has the Arcane trait, gain 1 Conviction.
>
> **Battle:** If the opponent loses, they reveal their hand. Send the card with the highest deckbuilding value to their Graveyard; choose among ties. If it has the Arcane trait, gain 1 Conviction.

If the opponent has no cards in hand, the effect does nothing.

### Heresy

**Cost:** 5  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Trait:** Arcane

> **Battle:** You may spend 4 Conviction to resolve the Battle effect of a card in the opponent's Graveyard as though you played it. That effect may resolve one additional Battle effect; the additional effect cannot resolve another.

The chosen card remains in the opponent's Graveyard.

### Hellfire

**Cost:** 5  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> **Action:** Spend any amount of Conviction. Send that many cards from the top of the opponent's deck to their Graveyard.
>
> **Battle:** After Battle cards are revealed, spend any amount of Conviction. For each Conviction spent, choose one: add +1 to your battle total; or, if you win, after the battle send the top card of the opponent's deck to their Graveyard. You may choose either option more than once.

Each Conviction provides only one chosen benefit.

## 7. Card-pool summary and deckbuilding reference

### Pool profile

| Cost | Cards |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Accusation |
| 2 | Confession; Penance; Divine Mercy |
| 3 | No Martyrs; Excommunication; Guilt by Association; Act of Faith |
| 4 | Tyranny; Burning at the Stake |
| 5 | Heresy; Hellfire |

**Total:** 12 unique cards; total unique-card value 37; average cost 3.08.

### Selecting cards

- **For a Grand Inquisitor deck:** consider Tyranny, Excommunication, Act of Faith, Burning at the Stake, and enough Conviction support to preserve Final Judgment after a battle win.
- **For a Witch Hunter deck:** consider Penance, Divine Mercy, No Martyrs, Tyranny, and conventional Battle effects while preserving the 2 Conviction needed for Relentless Pursuit.
- **For Purification pressure:** Accusation, Excommunication, Guilt by Association, Act of Faith, and Hellfire attack different deck structures rather than forming a mandatory package.
- **For coercive control:** Confession, Penance, Tyranny, and Burning at the Stake pressure hand commitments and premium cards.
- **For mixed plans:** use Purification as a threat that constrains the opponent while retaining enough immediate battle strength to win normally.

### Traits and copies

Heresy has the Arcane trait and therefore triggers opposing rules that refer to that trait, including an opposing Inquisition player's Blasphemy. No Inquisition card is Unique by default. All twelve cards use the normal v0.6 copy rules.

### Complexity

Basic cards are suitable for straightforward deck construction. Advanced cards introduce special reveal timing, negation, copied effects, multiple-zone selection, repeatable Assets, or loss-trigger suppression. Complexity is a player aid, not an additional in-game rule.

## Appendix A. Inquisition quick reference

### Conviction and doctrine

- **Maximum Conviction:** 4.
- **Normal gain:** first time each turn one or more opposing cards enter the Graveyard after a battle involving you, gain 1.
- **Condemnation:** opposing played battle-drawn cards go to the Graveyard after battles involving you.
- **Blasphemy:** whenever the opponent plays an Arcane-trait card, gain 1 Conviction outside the normal once-per-turn limit.
- **Purification:** after the opponent's normal start-of-turn draw attempt, if no card is drawn because deck and discard are empty, you win.

### Purge

| Cost | Purge |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Top discard card; or up to two discard cards with combined value 2 or less -> Graveyard. |
| 2 | One opposing Asset -> Graveyard. |
| 3 | Opponent chooses one hand card -> Graveyard. |
| 4 | Look at opponent's hand; choose one card -> Graveyard. |

### Leader abilities

- **Grand Inquisitor - Final Judgment:** once per turn after winning a battle, Purge immediately without using the Action opportunity; reduce the cost by 1, minimum 1.
- **Witch Hunter - Relentless Pursuit:** once per turn after an opponent loses a battle they initiated against you, spend 2 Conviction to end their turn, then move one space toward their Heartland; resolve an immediate battle if one begins.
