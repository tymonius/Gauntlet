# 1. Diplomat overview

The Diplomats win through controlled agreements, credible threats, and
political accumulation. Before a battle involving them, they may offer
Terms by selecting an eligible Proposal. Acceptance converts conflict
into a ratified Treaty Article; refusal creates a battle shaped by the
Proposal, available Influence, and the Diplomat card pool. Diplomats may
still run the Gauntlet, but their faction victory is the Peace Treaty.

## Faction components

- **Chosen Leader Card:** Ambassador or Senator. The card contains
  Setup, Leverage, the leader ability, and Peace Treaty victory.

- **Diplomat Influence Tracker:** Place it directly beneath the chosen
  Leader Card. Slide the Leader Card upward to show available Influence
  from 0 to 10.

- **Proposal / Treaty Article cards:** Nine double-sided cards. Begin
  Proposal side up; flip a newly ratified Proposal to its Treaty Article
  side.

- **Diplomat Reference:** One double-sided card summarizing Terms
  resolution, Influence, Leverage, and Treaty Articles.

- **Diplomat faction cards:** The twelve-card pool listed in Section 6.
  These are playable cards and use normal deckbuilding costs and copy
  rules unless a card says otherwise.

## Setup

- Choose Ambassador or Senator during deck construction.

- Place the selected Leader Card near your play area.

- Place the Influence Tracker beneath it and align the Leader Card at 1
  Influence.

- Place all nine Proposal cards Proposal side up and keep the Diplomat
  Reference available.

# 2. Influence and Terms

## Influence

Diplomats begin with 1 Influence and may have up to 10. Influence is
available political capital: it can be staked behind a Proposal, spent
through Leverage, gained through ratification or card effects, and lost
when refused Terms fail. Any gain above 10 is lost.

## Offering Terms

Before any battle involving you, choose one eligible Proposal. You must
satisfy its Requirement and have at least its listed Stake available.
Lower Influence by that Stake, then the opponent accepts or refuses the
Terms.

| **OUTCOME**            | **RESOLUTION**                                                                                                                                        |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Accepted**           | No battle occurs. Resolve the Accepted effect and return the Stake. If the Proposal is newly ratified, flip it and gain Influence equal to its Stake. |
| **Refused - you win**  | Resolve the battle. Impose the Proposal and return the Stake. If newly ratified, flip it and gain 1 Influence unless the Proposal says otherwise.     |
| **Refused - you lose** | Do not ratify the Proposal. Lose the Stake.                                                                                                           |
| **No winner**          | Do not ratify the Proposal. Return the Stake.                                                                                                         |

## Leverage

In a battle following refused Terms, before dice are rolled, you may
spend any amount of available Influence. Gain +1 to your battle total
for each Influence spent. Staked Influence is unavailable and cannot be
spent through Leverage.

## Ratification and Peace Treaty

- **New Article:** A Proposal becomes ratified only through accepted
  Terms or imposition after a Diplomat victory.

- **Repeated Proposal:** A ratified Proposal may be offered again for
  its tactical effect, but cannot be ratified again and grants no normal
  newly-ratified Influence reward.

- **Peace Treaty:** At the start of your turn, after captures, if five
  different Proposals are ratified, you win.

## Ambassador ability

| **ABILITY**    | **EFFECT**                                                           |
|----------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Cordiality** | Once per turn, after the opponent accepts your Terms, draw one card. |

## Ambassador priorities

- **Make acceptance credible:** Terms must offer something the opponent
  may rationally take. Cordiality rewards you only after the offer
  succeeds.

- **Preserve hand density:** Trade Concessions, Nonbinding Resolution,
  Good Faith, and Cordiality help the Ambassador keep enough cards to
  negotiate and still contest battle.

- **Sequence accepted effects:** Resolve the Proposal and all Terms
  modifiers before applying the final ratification and Influence
  rewards.

- **Avoid empty theater:** Repeated zero-Stake Proposals can be
  tactically useful, but they do not generate Influence merely because
  they are accepted.

## Senator ability

| **ABILITY**           | **EFFECT**                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Political Capital** | Once per turn, when you would lose staked Influence after losing a battle following refused Terms, you may send up to that many cards from hand to your Graveyard. Recover 1 staked Influence per card sent; lose the rest. |

## Senator priorities

- **Protect the right Stake:** Political Capital is once per turn.
  Saving 1 Influence now may leave a later two-Stake Proposal exposed.

- **Count the real cost:** Cards sent to the Graveyard do not return
  through ordinary reshuffling. Preserving Influence can permanently
  thin the deck.

- **Use partial recovery:** You may save only part of a lost Stake. One
  card can preserve 1 Influence from a two-Influence failure.

- **Keep enough cards to fight:** The Senator still needs cards for
  Battle commitments, Requirements, and Diplomat reactions. Political
  Capital is insurance, not free protection.

# 4. Playing Diplomats

## The central decision

<table>
<colgroup>
<col style="width: 100%" />
</colgroup>
<thead>
<tr class="header">
<th><p><strong>CONCESSION OR COERCION?</strong></p>
<p>Offer Terms the opponent might accept, while preserving enough
Influence and cards to make refusal dangerous. A Proposal that is
obviously harmless to refuse is not leverage; a Proposal that is too
costly to offer can leave the faction unable to act.</p></th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
</tbody>
</table>

## What Diplomats do well

- **Conflict conversion:** Accepted Terms replace battle with a
  controlled settlement, Treaty Article, and often a resource or
  card-flow benefit.

- **Refusal pressure:** Leverage, Gunboat Diplomacy, Neutral Observers,
  and Proposal-specific effects let refusal reshape the resulting
  battle.

- **Visible political accumulation:** Treaty Articles and Influence
  create two public clocks. Opponents can see both the path to victory
  and the resources available to force it.

- **Persistent pressure:** The Sanctions series punishes repeated
  refusal through Action friction, Asset restriction, and territorial
  movement pressure.

- **Leader flexibility:** The same Proposal and card pool supports an
  acceptance-focused Ambassador or a refusal-resistant Senator.

## What Diplomats do not do

- **No guaranteed agreement.** The opponent always retains the strategic
  choice to accept or refuse eligible Terms unless another rule
  explicitly says otherwise.

- **No free battle superiority.** Leverage costs Influence, and the
  current Stake is unavailable during the battle.

- **No unlimited card supply.** Many faction cards are held for
  out-of-turn timing. Poor sequencing can leave the Diplomat without a
  Battle commitment or Proposal Requirement.

- **No automatic fifth Article.** The final Proposal is public, and an
  opponent can force the Diplomat to impose it through battle.

- **No immunity to territorial defeat.** Treaty progress can continue
  while the board position worsens, but ordinary Heartland loss remains
  a threat.

## Building a Diplomat deck

The twelve-card pool is a negotiation toolkit, not a mandatory package.
Combine Proposal modifiers, accepted-Terms card flow, refusal tools,
Sanctions, and conventional Neutral support according to the kind of
bargain you want to present. A deck that contains only clever Terms
interactions may fail when it must actually win the refused battle.

## Counterplay

- **Evaluate the Article, not only the immediate bargain:** A favorable
  accepted effect may still advance the Diplomat toward the fifth Treaty
  Article.

- **Track available Influence:** A two-Stake Proposal reduces the
  Influence available for Leverage in that battle.

- **Force awkward sequencing:** Pressure the Diplomat hand so
  Requirements, reactions, Battle cards, and Political Capital compete
  for the same limited cards.

- **Use acceptance to lift Sanctions:** The three Sanctions remain until
  the named opponent later accepts Terms or the card-specific relief
  condition occurs.

# 5. Diplomat-specific rules reference

## Terms timing

- **Offer window:** Before any battle involving the Diplomat, before
  normal Battle-card commitments.

- **Eligibility:** The Proposal Requirement must be true and the
  Diplomat must have its listed Stake available.

- **One Proposal:** Offer one eligible Proposal unless Diplomatic
  Latitude explicitly allows two.

- **Stake once:** Lower available Influence by the listed Stake. That
  amount remains unavailable until the Terms resolve.

## Accepted Terms sequence

| **STEP** | **RESOLUTION**                                                                                             |
|----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**    | Resolve any card that modifies the offer or accepted choice.                                               |
| **2**    | Resolve the Proposal's Accepted effect. No battle occurs.                                                  |
| **3**    | Return the staked Influence.                                                                               |
| **4**    | If the Proposal is newly ratified, flip it to its Treaty Article side.                                     |
| **5**    | Gain Influence equal to its Stake, then resolve any after-acceptance leader or card trigger at its timing. |

## Refused Terms sequence

| **STEP** | **RESOLUTION**                                                                                          |
|----------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**    | Resolve the Proposal's Refused effect and any card that modifies the refused offer.                     |
| **2**    | Proceed through normal Battle-card commitments and battle draw.                                         |
| **3**    | Before dice, the Diplomat may spend available Influence through Leverage.                               |
| **4**    | Resolve the battle and determine whether the Diplomat wins, loses, or the battle ends without a winner. |
| **5**    | Apply imposition, Stake return or loss, ratification, and any newly-imposed reward.                     |

## Key terms

| **TERM**                | **MEANING**                                                                      |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Available Influence** | The value shown on the tracker; may be spent or staked.                          |
| **Staked Influence**    | Influence committed to the current Proposal and unavailable until Terms resolve. |
| **Newly ratified**      | A Proposal not already showing its Treaty Article side.                          |
| **Impose**              | Ratify a refused Proposal after the Diplomat wins the resulting battle.          |
| **Treaty Article**      | A ratified Proposal. Five different Articles satisfy Peace Treaty victory.       |
| **Sanction**            | A titled subseries of persistent Diplomat cards; not a separate core card type.  |

## Special rewards and limits

- **Ultimatum:** A newly imposed Ultimatum grants 2 Influence instead of
  the normal 1.

- **Diplomatic Recognition:** A newly imposed Diplomatic Recognition
  grants no Influence for imposition.

- **Accepted paid Proposals:** When newly ratified through acceptance,
  gain Influence equal to the Stake after returning it.

- **Already-ratified Proposals:** May be offered again, but provide no
  normal newly-ratified reward and do not count as another Article.

# 6. Canonical card pool

The following entries are the canonical v0.6 Diplomat names, costs, complexity, card forms, and exact player-facing text.

## Clemency

**Cost:** 1  
**Complexity:** Basic

> **Action:** Choose a card in the opponent's Graveyard. They choose one:
>
> - Move it to their discard pile; you gain 1 Influence.
> - Leave it there; you draw one card.

## Trade Concessions

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may reveal this from hand and set it aside.
>
> **Accepted:** The opponent chooses one available option:
>
> - Draw two cards.
> - Bank an Asset from hand without using an Action.
>
> Then discard this and draw one card.
>
> **Refused:** Return this to your hand before battle.
>
> **Battle:** The opponent draws one card. Gain +2 battle total.

## Safe Conduct

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Card form:** Asset

> **Action:** Bank this as an Asset.
>
> When you would lose a battle following refused Terms, you may discard this to withdraw instead. The opponent remains in or occupies the battle space, and the battle ends without a winner.

## Neutral Observers

**Cost:** 2  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Card form:** Asset

> **Action:** Bank this as an Asset.
>
> After Terms are refused, before either player commits from hand, you may discard this. The opponent first commits a Battle card from hand face up or declines to commit; then you commit normally.

## Good Faith

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Card form:** Asset

> **Action:** Bank this as an Asset.
>
> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may discard this to draw one card, then reveal a card from hand and set it aside.
>
> **Accepted:** Put that card in your Graveyard.
>
> **Refused:** Return it to your hand before battle.

## Demilitarized Zone

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Card form:** Territory Overlay

> After your Terms are accepted and the Proposal's Accepted effect resolves, you may play this on the battle Territory without using an Action. Each player still there withdraws.
>
> Neither player may enter this Territory during the turn this is placed.
>
> To enter it while unoccupied, discard one card.
>
> It cannot be captured or change control while this remains.
>
> At the start of your turn, before captures, if you occupy it, discard one card or withdraw.
>
> After the first battle here, discard this.

## Diplomatic Latitude

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> When you offer Terms, you may reveal this from hand and offer two eligible Proposals with the same Influence Stake instead of one. Stake that amount once.
>
> **Accepted:** The opponent chooses which Proposal is accepted.
>
> **Refused:** Choose one Proposal before resolving its Refused effect. Only it may be imposed.
>
> The other Proposal does not resolve or become ratified. After Terms resolve, discard this.

## Nonbinding Resolution

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may reveal this from hand and set it aside.
>
> **Accepted:** If the Proposal is unratified, the opponent chooses one before ratification:
>
> - Ratify it normally.
> - Leave it unratified; you gain 2 Influence.
>
> After the accepted Terms resolve, discard this, then draw one card.
>
> **Refused:** Discard this, then draw one card before battle.

## Sanctions: Censure

**Cost:** 3  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Card form:** Asset

> After an opponent refuses your Terms, you may bank this from hand without using an Action.
>
> While banked, the first time each turn that opponent plays an Action card, they choose: discard one card, or you draw one card.
>
> After they accept your Terms, discard this.

## Gunboat Diplomacy

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced

> When you offer Terms, before the opponent accepts or refuses, you may reveal this from hand.
>
> **Accepted:** Discard this.
>
> **Refused:** Play this face up in the resulting battle as an additional Battle card from hand.
>
> **Battle:** Gain +2 battle total. After the battle, discard this.

## Sanctions: Embargo

**Cost:** 4  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Card form:** Asset

> After an opponent refuses your Terms, you may bank this from hand without using an Action.
>
> While banked, that opponent's Asset-bank limit is reduced by 1, to a minimum of 0.
>
> After they accept your Terms, discard this.

## Sanctions: Blockade

**Cost:** 5  
**Complexity:** Advanced  
**Card form:** Territory Overlay

> After the battle following an opponent's refusal of your Terms, you may play this on a revealed Territory they control without using an Action.
>
> The first time each turn they enter or leave this Territory, they choose: discard one card, or you gain 1 Influence.
>
> Discard this after they accept your Terms or lose control of this Territory.

# 7. Card-pool summary and deckbuilding reference

## Pool profile

| **COST** | **CARDS**                                                                                      |
|----------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1**    | Clemency                                                                                       |
| **2**    | Trade Concessions; Safe Conduct; Neutral Observers                                             |

| **5**    | Sanctions: Blockade                                                                            |

Total: 12 unique cards; total unique-card value 35; average cost 2.92.

## Selecting cards

- **For an acceptance-focused Ambassador deck:** Trade Concessions, Good
  Faith, Nonbinding Resolution, Clemency, and Demilitarized Zone help
  convert accepted Terms into card flow, safety, and settlement value.

- **For a refusal-resistant Senator deck:** Safe Conduct, Neutral
  Observers, Gunboat Diplomacy, Diplomatic Latitude, and the Sanctions
  package help survive or punish refusal while Political Capital
  protects key Stakes.

- **For hand-density support:** Trade Concessions and Nonbinding
  Resolution replace themselves after accepted Terms; Good Faith
  replaces itself before setting another card aside; Clemency and
  Censure can generate cards conditionally.

- **For persistent pressure:** Censure taxes Actions, Embargo reduces
  Asset capacity, and Blockade pressures movement around a controlled
  Territory. All three offer relief through later accepted Terms.

- **For mixed plans:** Include conventional Battle strength and Neutral
  support. The faction must still survive refused battles and can still
  win by running the Gauntlet.

## Traits, copies, and complexity

The three Sanctions share a visible series name but remain ordinary
Assets or Territory Overlays as printed. No Diplomat card is Unique by
default. Basic and Advanced labels are player aids; all cards use normal
v0.6 copy rules unless later canonical data says otherwise.

## Package profile

The pool deliberately mixes negotiation modifiers, withdrawal safety,
card replacement, commitment control, settlement, visible Sanctions, and
one direct combat threat. It does not attempt to make every card useful
in every offer; the faction is built around selecting the right Proposal
and the right supporting card for the current political position.

# Appendix A. Diplomat quick reference

## Influence

| **INFLUENCE** | **RULE**                                                                                      |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Start**     | 1 Influence.                                                                                  |
| **Range**     | Minimum 0; maximum 10. Excess gains are lost.                                                 |
| **Stake**     | Lower the tracker by the Proposal's Stake. That Influence is unavailable until Terms resolve. |
| **Leverage**  | Before dice after refused Terms, spend available Influence for +1 battle total each.          |
| **Tracking**  | Slide the selected Leader Card over the shared 0-10 Influence Tracker.                        |

## Terms outcomes

| **OUTCOME**        | **STAKE** | **RATIFICATION**               | **NORMAL REWARD**                        |
|--------------------|-----------|--------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Accepted**       | Return    | If new, flip to Treaty Article | Gain Influence equal to Stake            |
| **Refused - win**  | Return    | If new, impose and flip        | Gain 1 Influence unless stated otherwise |
| **Refused - loss** | Lose      | No                             | None                                     |
| **No winner**      | Return    | No                             | None                                     |

## Leader abilities

| **LEADER**     | **ABILITY**       | **EFFECT**                                                                                                                                                               |
|----------------|-------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Ambassador** | Cordiality        | Once per turn, after the opponent accepts your Terms, draw one card.                                                                                                     |
| **Senator**    | Political Capital | Once per turn, when a refused battle loss would lose staked Influence, send up to that many cards from hand to your Graveyard; recover 1 staked Influence per card sent. |

## Five reminders

- Only available Influence can be spent through Leverage; the current
  Stake is unavailable.

- Accepted newly ratified Proposals return the Stake, then gain
  Influence equal to it.

- A ratified Proposal may be offered again, but cannot ratify or reward
  again normally.

- Sanctions are card-series titles, not a separate core card type.

- Peace Treaty is checked at the start of your turn after captures; five
  different Treaty Articles win.
