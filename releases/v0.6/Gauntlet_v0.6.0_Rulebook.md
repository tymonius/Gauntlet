# GAUNTLET

## Official Rulebook

**Version 0.6.0**

---

# Welcome to Gauntlet

Gauntlet is a two-player tactical card game of deck construction, strategy, timing, and territorial control. Before the game begins, each player builds a deck, chooses one of six factions and one of that faction's Leaders, and selects three Territories to form their side of the battlefield. Both players may choose the same faction or Leader.

Victory belongs to the player who successfully runs the Gauntlet. To do so, you must advance through a contested line of Territories, overcome your opponent's defenses, and defeat them in a final Last Stand beyond the end of the Gauntlet. Along the way, you will capture Territories, develop Assets, employ your faction's unique mechanics, and decide when your most valuable cards are worth committing permanently to battle.

The strength and composition of your deck shape every decision that follows. Careful construction gives you the tools to pursue a coherent plan, but victory also requires adapting that plan to the Territories, cards, and opportunities that emerge during play.

---

# Rules Conventions

- When a specific card, Leader, faction, or Territory rule conflicts with a general rule, follow the specific rule.
- **May** indicates an optional effect. **Must** indicates a required effect.
- Resolve instructions in the order written unless another rule specifies otherwise.
- Card text overrides a card's normal destination only to the extent stated.
- If an instruction cannot be completed in full, complete as much of it as possible unless the rule states otherwise.

## Playing Cards

A card may contain more than one printed effect. The effect that resolves depends on how the card is played.

- When you play a card during an **Action Opportunity**, resolve its **Action effect**.
- When you commit or play a card during a battle, resolve its **Battle effect**.
- If another rule instructs you to use a card in a different way, resolve the effect associated with that method.

Resolving one printed effect does not resolve any other printed effects on the same card unless a rule specifically states otherwise.

# 1. Components

Before beginning a game of Gauntlet, assemble a Deck and prepare the components needed to play.

## Each Player Needs

- One **Deck**
- One **Player Token**
- One six-sided die

Players may share a die if needed.

A **Deck** consists of:

- One **Faction**
- One **Leader Card**
- One **Playable Deck**
- Three different **Territory Cards**
- Any required faction- or Leader-specific supplemental cards or components

Some factions and Leaders include supplemental cards, reference cards, trackers, or other components used during play. These components are part of the Deck but are not part of the Playable Deck unless a rule explicitly states otherwise.

## The Gauntlet

Together, both players' Territory Cards form the **Gauntlet**, the six-Territory battlefield on which the game is played.

---

# 2. Building Your Deck

Every game of Gauntlet begins with a Deck assembled before play. Your Deck determines the cards, Leader, Territories, and faction abilities available to you throughout the game.

## Choose a Faction

Choose one faction. Your faction determines:

- the Leaders you may choose;
- the faction cards you may include in your Playable Deck; and
- any faction-specific rules or supplemental components you use during the game.

## Choose a Leader

Choose one Leader from your faction.

Each Leader has a unique ability that remains available throughout the game. Some Leaders include supplemental cards or other components required by their abilities. These components are part of that Leader's package and are not part of your Playable Deck.

## Build Your Playable Deck

Your **Playable Deck** is the constructed collection of cards used for normal drawing and card play.

A Playable Deck must contain:

- at least **30 cards**;
- no more than **60 total deckbuilding value**; and
- only Neutral cards and cards belonging to your chosen faction.

Unless marked **Unique**, a card may appear in any quantity permitted by the available card pool. A Unique card is limited to one copy in your Playable Deck.

## Choose Territory Cards

Choose exactly three different Territory Cards.

- No more than one may be an **Arena**.
- Territory Cards are not part of your Playable Deck.
- Territory Cards do not count toward the 30-card minimum or 60-value maximum.
- Both players may choose the same Territory titles.

## Prepare Supplemental Components

Prepare any cards, trackers, references, or other components required by your faction or Leader.

Unless a rule explicitly states otherwise, supplemental components:

- are not part of your Playable Deck;
- are not shuffled into your Draw Pile;
- cannot be drawn, played, discarded, banked, or sent to the Graveyard; and
- do not count toward deck size or deckbuilding value.

# 3. Setup

Prepare the game in the following order.

1. **Prepare your Draw Pile.** Shuffle your Playable Deck and place it face down to form your **Draw Pile**. Leave space beside it for your **Discard Pile** and **Graveyard**.
2. **Arrange your Territories.** Secretly arrange your three Territory Cards in a single line facing yourself.
3. **Form the Gauntlet.** Join both players' Territory lines to create one six-Territory column.
4. **Reveal the Territories.** Once both players have finished arranging them, reveal all six Territory Cards simultaneously. Territories remain face up for the rest of the game unless an effect states otherwise.
5. **Prepare Leaders and supplemental components.** Place your Leader Card face up and prepare any required faction- or Leader-specific components according to their rules.
6. **Place Player Tokens.** Place your Player Token immediately before the Territory at your end of the Gauntlet.
7. **Draw your opening hand.** Draw three cards from your Draw Pile.
8. **Determine the first player.** Both players roll their die. The player with the higher result takes the first turn. Reroll ties.

---

# 4. Turn Structure

Players alternate taking turns until one player wins the game.

Complete each turn in the following order:

1. **Capture**
2. **Draw**
3. **Action Opportunity before movement**
4. **Movement**
5. **Action Opportunity after movement**
6. **Cleanup**

## 1. Capture

At the start of your turn, if you occupy a Territory you do not control, capture it by rotating the Territory Card to face you.

## 2. Draw

Draw one card from your Draw Pile.

If your Draw Pile cannot complete the draw, shuffle your Discard Pile to form a new Draw Pile and continue drawing. If your Draw Pile and Discard Pile cannot provide the full number of cards, draw as many as possible.

## 3. Action Opportunity Before Movement

You may use your normal Action Opportunity before moving.

## 4. Movement

Advance, hold, or withdraw. Resolve any battle caused by movement immediately.

## 5. Action Opportunity After Movement

If you did not use your normal Action Opportunity before movement, you may use it now.

During your turn, you may normally play only one card for its Action effect, using either the Action Opportunity before movement or the Action Opportunity after movement. Some effects grant an additional Action Opportunity. Resolve those opportunities as instructed by the effect.

Whenever you could play a card for its Action effect, you may instead perform a rule or faction action that explicitly uses an Action Opportunity.

## 6. Cleanup

Resolve end-of-turn effects. If you have more than three cards in your hand, discard until you have three. Your turn then ends.

---

# 5. Movement

Movement changes the position of your Player Token. During your Movement step, choose one of the following:

- **Advance:** Move one position toward your opponent's end of the Gauntlet.
- **Hold:** Remain in your current position.
- **Withdraw:** Move one position toward your end of the Gauntlet.

You cannot voluntarily withdraw beyond your end of the Gauntlet. Players cannot move through or past one another.

A **position** is any location a Player Token may occupy: a Territory, the position before either end of the Gauntlet, or the position beyond either end of the Gauntlet.

If an effect grants additional movement, resolve each movement one position at a time unless the effect states otherwise.

## Entering an Occupied Territory

When you advance into a Territory occupied by your opponent, move your Player Token into that Territory before resolving the battle.

Although both Player Tokens are physically in the Territory, the defending player continues to occupy it until the battle is resolved. If the attacking player wins, the defending player retreats and the attacking player then occupies the Territory.

---

# 6. Battles

Battles occur when opposing players contest the same position. Winning a battle may allow a player to occupy an enemy-controlled Territory or continue toward the end of the Gauntlet.

## When a Battle Begins

A battle begins when:

- a player advances into a Territory occupied by the opponent;
- a player advances beyond the final Territory to battle an opponent who has been forced beyond their end of the Gauntlet; or
- a card or rule explicitly begins a battle.

The player whose movement or effect began the battle is the **attacking player**. The other player is the **defending player**.

## Resolving a Battle

Resolve each battle in the following order.

### 1. Commit a Card

Each player may secretly commit one card from their hand for its Battle effect by placing it face down.

### 2. Form Battle Hands

Each player draws three cards to form their **Battle Hand**. A Battle Hand is temporary for the current battle and is separate from the player's normal hand.

### 3. Choose a Card from the Battle Hand

Each player may choose one card from their Battle Hand for its Battle effect and place it face down.

### 4. Reveal Cards

Reveal all committed and chosen cards simultaneously.

### 5. Resolve Battle Effects

Resolve the revealed Battle effects according to their text and applicable timing rules.

### 6. Roll Dice

Each player rolls one six-sided die and applies all relevant effects and modifiers to determine their battle total.

### 7. Determine the Winner

The player with the higher battle total wins.

**Defender's Advantage:** If the battle totals are tied and the defending player controls the contested Territory, the defending player wins. The defending player also has Defender's Advantage during their Last Stand.

If a tie is not resolved by Defender's Advantage or another effect, reroll the battle dice until one player wins.

### 8. Resolve the Outcome

The losing player retreats one position away from the winning player unless an effect changes the retreat.

If the attacking player wins a battle on a Territory, the attacking player occupies that Territory after the defending player retreats. The Territory remains under its current controller until it is captured.

## Battle Cleanup

After resolving the battle:

- place each card committed from a normal hand in its owner's Graveyard; and
- discard every card in each Battle Hand, including any card played from it, unless an effect specifies another destination.

---

# 7. Territory Control

Winning a battle allows a player to occupy a Territory. Holding it until the start of that player's next turn allows the player to capture it.

## Control

The player a Territory Card faces controls that Territory. Control does not require the controlling player's token to remain there.

Each player begins the game controlling the three Territories on their side of the Gauntlet. Control changes only through capture or a rule that explicitly changes it.

## Occupying a Territory

Winning a battle on a Territory allows the winner to occupy it after the losing player retreats.

Occupation and control are not the same. A player may occupy a Territory controlled by the opponent.

## Capturing a Territory

At the start of your turn, if you occupy a Territory you do not control, capture it by rotating the Territory Card to face you.

A captured Territory remains under your control until your opponent captures it or an effect changes control.

## Counterattacks

Because a Territory is not captured immediately after a battle, its controller has an opportunity to drive the occupying opponent away before the start of that opponent's next turn.

---

# 8. Running the Gauntlet

To win by running the Gauntlet, advance through the full column of Territories, force the opponent beyond their end of the Gauntlet, and win their Last Stand.

## Forcing the Opponent Beyond the Gauntlet

When a player loses a battle while occupying the final Territory at their end of the Gauntlet, that player retreats beyond the Territory column.

The winning player occupies the final Territory. The battle then ends normally. The winning player does not immediately advance beyond the Gauntlet.

If the winning player still occupies that final Territory at the start of their next turn, they capture it during the Capture step. During that turn's Movement step, they may advance beyond the final Territory to initiate the opponent's Last Stand.

## Last Stand

When a player advances beyond the final Territory to battle an opponent who has been forced beyond their end of the Gauntlet, that battle is the defending player's **Last Stand**.

During a Last Stand:

- the defending player has **Defender's Advantage**; and
- the defending player gains **+1** to their battle total.

If the attacking player wins the Last Stand, that player has run the Gauntlet and immediately wins the game.

If the defending player wins, the attacking player retreats to the final Territory. Play continues normally.

Some factions provide an additional victory condition. Those conditions are described in the corresponding faction section.

# 9. Game Zones

## Draw Pile

The face-down pile formed by shuffling your Playable Deck during setup.

## Hand

Your private cards available for Action effects, Battle commitment, and other effects. The normal hand limit is three.

## Battle Hand

A temporary hand of three cards drawn for a battle. It exists only for that battle and is separate from your normal hand.

## Discard Pile

A face-up pile of recyclable cards. When your Draw Pile cannot complete a draw, shuffle your Discard Pile to form a new Draw Pile.

## Graveyard

A face-up pile of cards removed from normal circulation. Cards in the Graveyard are not reshuffled unless an effect explicitly moves them.

## Asset Bank

The public area containing your banked Assets.

## Supplemental Areas

Some factions and effects create additional areas. These areas follow the rules that create them.

---

# 10. Actions, Assets, and Overlays

## Action Effects

To play a card for its Action effect:

1. play it from your hand during an Action Opportunity;
2. satisfy any stated cost or requirement;
3. resolve its Action effect; and
4. discard it unless it becomes an Asset or Overlay or specifies another destination.

Whenever you could play a card for its Action effect, you may instead perform a rule or faction action that explicitly uses an Action Opportunity.

## Assets

A card is an Asset only when an effect banks it in the Asset Bank.

Your Asset limit equals the number of Territories you control.

- A Territory you occupy but do not control does not increase your Asset limit.
- If your Asset limit falls below the number of Assets in your Asset Bank, immediately choose and discard Assets until you are within the limit.
- An inactive Asset still counts toward your Asset limit unless a rule says otherwise.

Whenever you could play a card for its Action effect, you may instead discard one Asset you control unless an effect prohibits it.

## Overlays

An Overlay is a persistent card placed over a Territory. It modifies or replaces that Territory's printed effect as stated on the card.

Unless stated otherwise:

- only one Overlay may be attached to a Territory;
- placing a new Overlay on a Territory discards the previous Overlay;
- an Overlay is not an Asset and does not count toward the Asset limit; and
- an Overlay leaves play when its own rule or another effect removes it.

# 12. Glossary

**Action Opportunity:** A point during your turn when you may play a card for its Action effect or perform another rule or faction action that explicitly uses the opportunity.

**Advance:** Move one position toward the opponent's end of the Gauntlet.

**Asset:** A card banked in a player's Asset Bank.

**Asset Bank:** The public area containing a player's Assets.

**Battle Hand:** A temporary hand of cards drawn for a battle, separate from the player's normal hand.

**Capture:** Gain control of a Territory you occupy by rotating it to face you at the start of your turn.

**Control:** A player controls a Territory when the Territory Card faces that player.

**Deck:** A player's complete pregame package, consisting of a faction, Leader, Playable Deck, three Territory Cards, and any required supplemental components.

**Defender's Advantage:** The defending player wins a tied battle total when they control the contested Territory or are defending during their Last Stand.

**Draw Pile:** The face-down pile formed by shuffling a Playable Deck during setup.

**Graveyard:** A face-up pile of cards removed from normal circulation.

**Hold:** Remain in the current position during movement.

**Last Stand:** The final battle fought beyond a player's end of the Gauntlet after that player has been forced beyond the Territory column.

**Mission:** An Intelligence supplemental card used according to the Intelligence faction rules.

**Occupy:** Hold a position. During an unresolved attack on an occupied Territory, the defending player continues to occupy that Territory.

**Playable Deck:** The constructed collection of cards used for normal drawing and card play.

**Position:** Any location a Player Token may occupy.

**Retreat:** Forced movement one position away from the winning player after losing a battle, unless an effect changes it.

**Run the Gauntlet:** Advance through the Territory column and win the opponent's Last Stand.

**Territory:** One of the six cards forming the Gauntlet.

**Withdraw:** Voluntarily move one position toward your end of the Gauntlet.

# Quick Reference

## Setup

1. Shuffle your Playable Deck to form your Draw Pile.
2. Secretly arrange your three Territories.
3. Join both Territory lines and reveal all six simultaneously.
4. Prepare your Leader and supplemental components.
5. Place your Player Token before your end of the Gauntlet.
6. Draw three cards.
7. Roll to determine the first player; reroll ties.

## Turn

1. Capture a Territory you occupy but do not control.
2. Draw one card.
3. Use your Action Opportunity before movement, if desired.
4. Advance, hold, or withdraw; resolve any battle.
5. Use your Action Opportunity after movement if the first was unused.
6. Resolve end-of-turn effects and discard down to three cards.

## Battle

1. Optionally commit one card from your hand for its Battle effect.
2. Draw three cards to form your Battle Hand.
3. Optionally choose one card from your Battle Hand for its Battle effect.
4. Reveal played cards.
5. Resolve Battle effects.
6. Roll and determine battle totals.
7. Apply Defender's Advantage where applicable.
8. The losing player retreats.
9. Place committed hand cards in the Graveyard; discard all Battle Hand cards.
